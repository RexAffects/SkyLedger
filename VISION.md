## Why We Ask for Photos — The Ledger

When you upload a photo, you're not just sharing a picture. You're creating a legal artifact.

Your phone embeds invisible data into every photo: GPS coordinates (where you stood), a timestamp (when you took it), and a device ID (which camera captured it). The moment you upload, Sky Ledger generates a SHA-256 cryptographic hash — a digital fingerprint that proves the photo was never altered after submission.

### How Matching Works — Candidate Pools, Not Accusations

We don't pin reports to aircraft unless the evidence is airtight. Instead, every report generates a **candidate pool** — the set of aircraft that were near you at the time of your photo, ranked by likelihood.

The system presents: "These aircraft were in the area at the time of your observation." Not: "This aircraft did it." The difference matters. We don't throw accusations. We present data and let accumulation do the talking.

When you submit in real time — while the aircraft is still airborne — we capture a snapshot of every aircraft overhead. If only one is at cloud-seeding altitude and it's a known weather modification operator, that's a very strong candidate. If it's a real-time confirmed match, that's gold-tier evidence: citizen photo → aircraft → owner → accountability.

When you submit later, the plane has landed, but your photo still carries its timestamp and GPS in the metadata. Using historical flight data, we can retroactively build the candidate pool — "which aircraft were near this location at this time?" — and rank them the same way.

### Undeniable Through Accumulation

Here's where it gets powerful. A single report with three candidates proves nothing about any one of them. But when the SAME aircraft keeps appearing in candidate pools — across different citizens, different cities, different days — the data starts speaking for itself.

An aircraft that appears in 47 different citizen report pools, from 23 independent reporters, across 12 locations, and has been real-time confirmed 8 times? Nobody needs to accuse that aircraft. The ledger did it.

Meanwhile, the Delta flight that happened to pass overhead once? It appeared in one pool and never again. Cumulative score: zero.

This approach protects everyone:
- **Innocent aircraft** are never falsely flagged — one appearance doesn't mean anything.
- **Bad actors can't game the system** — 50 reports from one person count less than 5 reports from 5 different people.
- **Sky Ledger stays credible** — we present data, not verdicts. The accumulation speaks for itself.
- **It gets stronger over time** — even after raw flight data ages out, the candidate pool records persist. The cumulative evidence keeps compounding.

### Why Every Report Matters — Even Without a Match

Not every report needs to identify a specific aircraft for the ledger to be powerful:

- **Volume proves the phenomenon.** A legislator doesn't need tail numbers. They need 500 timestamped, geolocated, hashed photos from their district showing persistent trails. That's constituent evidence that says "this is happening and your voters see it."
- **Patterns reveal operators.** If reports cluster on the same days, same times, same corridors — that's an operational schedule. Schedules have operators. Operators have contracts. You work backwards from the pattern even without a single aircraft match.
- **Matched reports validate unmatched ones.** If 10% of reports pin to a specific operator, and the other 90% show the same time/location/behavior patterns, the matched reports confirm what the unmatched ones are pointing to.
- **The map is visceral.** When someone visits Sky Ledger and sees thousands of pins, each with a citizen photo, that's not anecdotal. That's undeniable. The visual weight of a filled map does more for public awareness than any single matched report.
- **The community gets smarter over time.** More people watching = more real-time reports = more matches. The ledger trains the community to look up and report. The real-time match rate climbs as adoption grows. It's a flywheel.

### The Three Tiers of Evidence

Every report contributes, but at different levels:

- **Gold — Real-time match.** Submitted while the aircraft is airborne. ADS-B snapshot captured. Aircraft identified. Owner linked. Court-ready.
- **Silver — Historical match.** Submitted later, but EXIF timestamp and GPS allow retroactive flight data lookup. Same strength, just delayed. (Grows as we integrate historical data.)
- **Bronze — Unmatched.** No aircraft identified. But the report is timestamped, geolocated, and hashed. Documents the phenomenon. Feeds pattern detection. Supports legislative pressure through volume.

All three tiers are valuable. Gold wins court cases. Silver will too. Bronze wins legislative battles and builds the pattern database that makes gold and silver possible.

### The Flip: From Passive to Active

The ultimate evolution: instead of hoping people report in real time, we TELL them when to look up. Push alerts when a known weather modification operator enters a user's area — "N12345 (owned by WeatherMod Inc) is currently near your location at 12,000ft. See anything? Report now." This flips passive reporting into active tracking and dramatically increases the real-time match rate.

### Why It's Called Sky Ledger

Every report is an entry in an accountability ledger:
- **When** it happened (photo timestamp)
- **Where** it was observed (photo GPS)
- **What** was seen (the photo itself, hashed and tamper-proof)
- **Who** was flying (when matched: ADS-B → tail number → FAA owner)
- **Who else saw it** (cross-report correlation from independent observers)
- **What the pattern shows** (time/location/behavior clustering across all reports)

This data is built to legal standards. Federal Rule of Evidence 901 requires digital evidence to be authenticated — shown to be what its proponent claims. Our SHA-256 hashing, EXIF preservation, and ADS-B corroboration satisfy that standard. FAA registration data is federal public record — inherently admissible.

Your photo isn't just a photo. It's an entry in a ledger that grows stronger with every contributor. And when the patterns become undeniable, no one can dismiss what thousands of independent citizens documented with their own eyes and their own cameras.
