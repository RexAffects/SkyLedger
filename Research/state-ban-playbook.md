# How States Ban Geoengineering: The Playbook

*Derived from analysis of Tennessee (2024), Florida (2025), and Louisiana (2025) — the three states that have enacted laws.*

---

## The Pattern: What All Three Did

Every successful ban followed the same five-phase sequence. The details varied, but the structure didn't.

### Phase 1: Frame It Right

**The single most important decision is how you talk about it.**

All three states that succeeded avoided "chemtrails" in their bill text. Every bill that failed or got mocked led with conspiracy language on the floor.

| What worked | What didn't |
|---|---|
| "Protecting our air, water, and soil" | "Chemtrails are real" |
| "State sovereignty / Tenth Amendment" | "They're spraying us" |
| "Precautionary — drive a stake in the ground" | Leading with photos of contrails |
| "The government's own documents say they're planning this" | Unverifiable personal claims |
| "Informed consent — no one asked us" | Angry accusations without citations |

**The killer move in all three states:** Citing the **June 2023 White House OSTP report** on Solar Radiation Modification. This is a federal government document acknowledging interest in atmospheric intervention. It forces opponents into a corner — they can't dismiss the premise when the White House itself published a research plan.

Other powerful citations:
- **Make Sunsets** — a startup that actually released sulfur dioxide into the atmosphere (2023-2024)
- A **2024 outdoor cloud-brightening test** that was shut down over health concerns
- **NOAA weather modification project reports** — prove this isn't theoretical
- The **GAO audit** finding NOAA's oversight is inadequate

### Phase 2: Find Your Sponsor

**The sponsor's background matters enormously.**

| State | Sponsor | Why they worked |
|---|---|---|
| Tennessee | Rep. Monty Fritts | Chemistry degree + nuclear industry background. Could speak credibly about chemical exposure routes. |
| Florida | Sen. Ileana Garcia | Cuban-American, former DHS deputy press secretary. Framed it as protecting freedom, not conspiracy. |
| Louisiana | Sen. "Big Mike" Fesi | Oil & gas businessman. Understood regulatory gaps. No-nonsense credibility. |

**What to look for in a sponsor:**
- Science, military, agriculture, or business background (not just "activist legislator")
- Already has credibility with the caucus on other issues
- Willing to stay disciplined on framing (no freelancing into conspiracy territory on the floor)
- Ideally chairs or sits on the committee the bill will go through

### Phase 3: Organize the Pressure Campaign

**This is where bills live or die.** All three states had massive constituent contact campaigns:

| State | Volume | Primary organizer |
|---|---|---|
| Tennessee | 11,163 emails to committee members | Tennessee Stands |
| Florida | ~100,000 emails over 3 months | Stand for Health Freedom + Global Wellness Forum |
| Louisiana | 400+ reports filed immediately after passage | Organic constituent interest |

**The infrastructure that makes this work:**

1. **Stand for Health Freedom** (standforhealthfreedom.com) — provides a platform where supporters enter their info and it auto-routes emails/calls to the correct legislators. Used in TN and FL. Contact them about your state.

2. **Zero Geoengineering** (zerogeoengineering.com) — provides **model legislation** (the actual bill text template). Jolie Diane has been getting bills introduced since 2018. She was credited by Tennessee's organizers as "an amazing wealth of information."

3. **Existing medical freedom / health freedom networks** — Tennessee's organizers explicitly said they repurposed connections built during COVID medical freedom fights. The same people who organized against mandates pivoted to geoengineering. Quote from Danielle Goodrich (East Tennessee Freedom): *"We used the connections and the rapport that we had built over the last couple of years in medical freedom."*

4. **Children's Health Defense state chapters** — RFK Jr.'s organization. Active in TN, promoted testimony and amplified the bill.

**Testimony strategy:**
- Bring **credentialed witnesses**: physicians, licensed healthcare providers, scientists. Dr. Denise Sibley (internal medicine physician) testified in both TN chambers. Marla Maples testified in FL.
- Keep testimony focused on: the White House OSTP report, the regulatory vacuum, informed consent, environmental protection
- Coach witnesses: *"Please be respectful in your communications. This is not personal."* (Tennessee Stands guidance)

### Phase 4: Navigate the Legislature

**Speed matters.** Tennessee went from filing to signature in 11 weeks. The faster the bill moves, the less time opposition has to organize.

**Procedural tactics that worked:**

- **Caption bill strategy (TN):** File a placeholder bill on a minor topic, then amend it with the real language later. This provides procedural flexibility.
- **Two-bill strategy (LA):** File a broader, more aggressive bill alongside a narrower one. The aggressive bill (HB 608, $200K fine) absorbs criticism and fails 21-72, making the narrower bill (SB 46) look reasonable by comparison.
- **Governor alignment (FL):** DeSantis publicly pressured the House when they tried to gut the bill, forcing them to pass the stronger Senate version. Having the governor on your side early is a force multiplier.

**Handling opposition:**

Opposition will come from three directions:
1. **Democrats calling it conspiracy** — Let them. The Sasquatch amendment in TN and "touch grass" comments in FL/LA actually energized supporters and made opponents look dismissive.
2. **Scientists saying it's not happening** — Redirect: "If it's not happening, this bill costs nothing. If it is, we're protected." Tennessee's own Fiscal Review Committee said the impact was "not significant" because the activity isn't occurring — and the bill still passed.
3. **Cloud seeding industry** — Rainmaker testified in LA. This is the only substantive opposition. Decide in advance whether your bill exempts existing agricultural cloud seeding or bans everything (TN banned everything; FL repealed the entire permit framework).

**The asymmetric political cost:**
In a red state, voting YES costs nothing (bans something that "isn't happening" anyway). Voting NO means appearing to support the federal government's right to spray chemicals on your constituents. No legislator wants that attack ad.

### Phase 5: Manage the Media

**National media ridicule helps you.** This is counterintuitive but was true in all three states.

- NBC, Washington Post, PolitiFact all ran critical stories about Tennessee's bill. It passed 70-22.
- Florida Phoenix called SB 56 "ludicrous." It passed 82-28.
- Louisiana Illuminator called the bills "fueled by magic and conspiracy theories." SB 46 passed 58-33.

**Why it helps:** Coverage activates the conservative base (distrust of mainstream media), gives the bill enormous visibility, and creates a culture-war dynamic where voting against the bill means siding with the media.

**What to do with media:**
- Don't seek mainstream media approval. You won't get it.
- Do cultivate conservative/alternative media and local talk radio
- The sponsor should give clean, quotable statements focused on environmental protection and sovereignty, not conspiracy
- Have supporters share coverage (even critical coverage) on social media — it drives engagement

## The Universal Checklist: Bring This to Your State

### 6-9 Months Before Session

- [ ] **Get model legislation** from Zero Geoengineering (zerogeoengineering.com)
- [ ] **Identify your sponsor** — look for a legislator with science/military/ag/business credentials who already champions sovereignty or environmental issues
- [ ] **Map your committee pathway** — know which committee the bill will be referred to and who chairs it
- [ ] **Contact Stand for Health Freedom** to set up an action alert for your state
- [ ] **Build your coalition** — connect with existing medical freedom groups, health freedom networks, CHD state chapter, local liberty groups
- [ ] **Research your state's regulatory vacuum** — most states have no weather modification law. "There's nothing protecting us" is a powerful argument.

### 3-6 Months Before Session

- [ ] **Draft the bill** with your sponsor's legislative counsel, using the model legislation as a starting point
- [ ] **Decide on scope**: ban everything (TN model) or carve out agricultural exemptions?
- [ ] **Decide on penalties**: misdemeanor (TN: $10K/day) or felony (FL: $100K + 5 years)?
- [ ] **Build your witness list** for committee testimony — physicians, scientists, healthcare providers with credentials
- [ ] **Pre-file the bill** if your state allows it
- [ ] **Begin the email/contact campaign** to committee members

### During Session

- [ ] **Flood committee members with constituent emails** before the first hearing (target: thousands)
- [ ] **Pack the committee hearing** with supporters
- [ ] **Coordinate testimony** — lead with the White House OSTP report, NOAA data, and the regulatory gap. Close with informed consent.
- [ ] **Have your sponsor stay on-message** — environmental protection, state sovereignty, precautionary principle
- [ ] **Don't engage with mockery** — let opponents mock. It helps you.
- [ ] **Move fast** — the longer a bill sits, the more time opposition has to organize

### After Passage

- [ ] **Push for a reporting mechanism** — LA required DEQ to collect citizen reports. This creates ongoing engagement and data.
- [ ] **Document the process** for other states
- [ ] **Connect with the national network** — your win becomes a proof point for the next state
