## 1. It's Legal and Actively Happening

- **9 states actively operate cloud seeding programs** as of 2025: California, Colorado, Idaho, Nevada, New Mexico, North Dakota, Texas, Utah, and Wyoming. ([Source: TDLR](https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/weather/weatherfaq.htm))

- **The Weather Modification Reporting Act of 1972** (Public Law 92-205) requires all persons conducting weather modification in the U.S. to report activities to the Secretary of Commerce at least 10 days prior to and after operations. Failure to report: up to $10,000 fine. ([Source: NOAA](https://library.noaa.gov/weather-climate/weather-modification-project-reports))

- **NOAA has collected 832+ weather modification activity reports** from 2000-2025. A structured dataset was published in *Scientific Data* (Nature) in 2025. ([Source: Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-025-06273-1))

- **Texas has 5 active cloud seeding programs** covering approximately one-sixth of the state's total area. State-funded from 1997-2004 ($11.7 million in matching funds). Now funded by local sponsors. Regulated by the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation under the Texas Weather Modification Act (1967). ([Source: TDLR](https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/weather/summary.htm))

- **Colorado has a state-run Weather Modification Program** operated by the Colorado Water Conservation Board (CWCB). ([Source: CWCB](https://cwcb.colorado.gov/focus-areas/supply/weather-modification-program))

## 3. The Government Admits Oversight Is Broken

- **GAO Report GAO-26-108013 (February 23, 2026):** "Weather Modification: NOAA Should Strengthen Oversight to Ensure Reliable Information." Key findings:
  - NOAA has overseen weather modification reporting for **50+ years** but is **not fully meeting its responsibilities**
  - **Over half of all reports filed with NOAA likely have errors**, including missing required information
  - NOAA has **no written guidance** for reviewing reports or maintaining its database
  - Reporting forms have been **virtually unchanged since 1974**
  - NOAA officials told GAO they **make no effort to seek out unreported weather modification experiments**
  - ([Source: GAO](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-26-108013))

- **EPA Administrator Lee Zeldin** publicly stated: "Americans have urgent questions about geoengineering. That era [of dismissing questions] is over." Ordered EPA to compile and publish everything they know about contrails and geoengineering.

## 5. States Are Passing Laws to Ban It

- **Tennessee SB 2691** — Signed into law April 11, 2024 by Governor Bill Lee. First state to ban geoengineering. Prohibits intentional injection, release, or dispersion of chemicals into the atmosphere to affect temperature, weather, or sunlight intensity. Violations: Class C misdemeanor, up to $10,000/day per violation. ([Source: LegiScan](https://legiscan.com/TN/bill/SB2691/2023))

- **Florida SB 56** — Passed 2025, signed by Governor DeSantis. Prohibits weather modification activities including cloud seeding and solar radiation modification. **Felony penalties.** Created a public complaint system for citizens. ([Source: Florida Phoenix](https://floridaphoenix.com/2025/04/03/florida-senate-approves-ban-on-geoengineering-and-weather-modification/))

- **Montana SB 473** — Passed March 6, 2025. Bans stratospheric aerosol injection and solar radiation management. Permits cloud seeding for water resource management.

- **Louisiana Act No. 95** — Created a state reporting portal for weather modification activities.

- **22+ states** introduced geoengineering ban bills in 2025. ([Source: SRM360](https://srm360.org/news-reaction/us-states-consider-bills-to-ban-geoengineering/))

- **H.R. 4403 — Clear Skies Act** (Federal) — Introduced by Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene. Would ban all weather modification nationwide, repeal all existing federal authority permitting it, create a public reporting system through EPA. Criminal penalties: up to $100,000 fine and 5 years imprisonment. ([Source: Congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/4403/text))

- **The Solar Geoengineering Non-Use Agreement** — launched January 17, 2022 by 60+ senior climate scientists. Now signed by **600+ academics** and **~2,000 civil society organizations.** Published as a peer-reviewed paper: Biermann et al., 2022 in *WIREs Climate Change.* ([Source: solargeoeng.org](https://www.solargeoeng.org/))

- **Full list of every signatory** — name, institution, field — is publicly available: [solargeoeng.org/non-use-agreement/signatories/](https://www.solargeoeng.org/non-use-agreement/signatories/)

- **The open letter** calling for an international non-use agreement: [solargeoeng.org/non-use-agreement/open-letter/](https://www.solargeoeng.org/non-use-agreement/open-letter/)

- **Five demands:**
  1. Prohibit national funding agencies from supporting SG development
  2. Ban outdoor experiments of solar geoengineering technologies
  3. Do not grant patent rights for solar geoengineering technologies
  4. No public funding for deployment
  5. Object to future institutionalization of SG in international institutions

- Countries across **Africa, Europe, and the Pacific** have signaled support. EU advisors recommended pushing for a global non-use deal.

- **Stardust Solutions is doing the exact opposite of every measure** — conducting outdoor experiments, seeking patents, pursuing commercialization, and building deployment capacity.

## 9. Termination Shock — The Science

- **If stratospheric aerosol deployment is started and then stopped, temperatures spike at 5-10x the rate of normal global warming.** This is called "termination shock." ([Source: Parker & Irvine, 2018, *Earth's Future* (AGU)](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/2017EF000735))

- Aerosol particles last ~1-2 years in the stratosphere. Deployment must be **continuous and perpetual** — stopping at any point causes the masked warming to return rapidly.

- A real-world example: In 2020, fuel regulations reduced shipping sulfur dioxide emissions by ~80%. This created an **inadvertent termination shock** with measurable radiative warming. Published in *Nature Communications Earth & Environment* (2024). ([Source: Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43247-024-01442-3))

- There is scientific debate about the **severity** — some researchers argue the risk has been "significantly overestimated." But the fundamental physics is not disputed: stop spraying → warming returns.

- **The business implication:** Any government that starts paying for SAI deployment is locked into perpetual contracts. Stopping = catastrophic temperature spike. This creates permanent dependency on whoever holds the patent — a perpetual revenue stream.
