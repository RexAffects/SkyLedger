# Arkansas Geoengineering Ban: Game Plan

*Target: January 2027 Regular Session*
*Last updated: March 23, 2026*

---

## Current Situation

**Arkansas is an open field.** No bill has ever been introduced. No prior failure to explain away. No organized opposition. The political conditions are as favorable as they get:

- **Republican supermajority**: House 81-19, Senate 29-6
- **Republican governor** (Sarah Huckabee Sanders) with a track record of challenging federal overreach (sued EPA)
- **No existing weather modification law** — Arkansas is one of ~21 states with zero regulation. The regulatory vacuum IS the argument.
- **No active cloud seeding programs** — NOAA has zero weather modification reports for Arkansas. Voting yes costs nothing.
- **Agricultural state** — "protect Arkansas farmland from unregulated atmospheric experiments" resonates deeply

**What exists:**
- A Change.org petition: "Halt All Cloud Seeding and Weather Modification Practices in Arkansas" — proves grassroots interest
- Zero Geoengineering has an Arkansas state page (zerogeoengineering.com/us-arkansas/) — they're tracking us
- ACA 12-75-115 is the only statutory reference to weather modification (governor's emergency powers, disaster prevention context)
- In 2020, governor's emergency powers were quietly expanded to include weather modification authority — no public debate

**What doesn't exist:**
- No Arkansas-specific anti-geoengineering organization
- No identified legislator who has taken a public position
- No local media coverage of the issue as an Arkansas story

## The Game Plan

### Phase 1: Foundation (April–August 2026)

**Goal: Build the coalition and identify the sponsor before anyone writes a word of legislation.**

#### 1.1 Find the Sponsor

This is the single most important step. The wrong sponsor kills the bill before it starts.

**What to look for:**
- Republican (obviously, given the supermajority)
- Science, military, agriculture, or business background — NOT primarily known as a culture warrior
- Sits on or chairs the **Agriculture, Forestry & Economic Development Committee** (House or Senate) — this is the ideal committee pathway
- Alternatively: **Public Health, Welfare, and Labor Committee**
- Has demonstrated willingness to buck federal authority on other issues
- Willing to stay disciplined on framing

**How to find them:**
- Review the committee rosters at arkleg.state.ar.us/Committees/List
- Look for members from rural/agricultural districts — they'll be most receptive to the farmland protection framing
- Check voting records on sovereignty/states' rights bills
- Attend interim committee meetings (these happen between sessions) and build relationships

**Approach strategy:**
Don't lead with "chemtrails." Lead with:
> "Senator/Representative, I've been studying what Tennessee, Florida, and Louisiana have done. The federal government published a research plan for atmospheric modification. Three states have already banned it. Arkansas has zero protections. I'd like to show you what a bill looks like."

Bring:
- A printed copy of the White House OSTP report (June 2023)
- A one-page summary of TN/FL/LA laws
- The model legislation from Zero Geoengineering
- The fact that Arkansas has NO weather modification law and the governor's emergency powers were expanded to include it in 2020 with no public debate

#### 1.2 Build the Coalition

**National organizations to contact immediately:**

1. **Zero Geoengineering** (director@zerogeoengineering.com) — Request the model legislation and ask about Arkansas-specific strategy. Jolie Diane has been doing this since 2018 and was credited by Tennessee's organizers.

2. **Stand for Health Freedom** (standforhealthfreedom.com) — Ask them to set up an Arkansas action alert. Their platform is what generated 11K emails in TN and 100K in FL. This is infrastructure you don't have to build.

3. **The GeoFight** (thegeofight.com) — Nicole Pearson, Blake Horwitz, Tom Renz. Their federal legal strategy complements state legislation. Ask if they have any Arkansas connections.

4. **Children's Health Defense** — Check if there's an Arkansas chapter. If not, find the nearest state chapter and ask about expansion.

**Local organizations to connect with:**

- Medical freedom / health freedom groups in Arkansas (these were the backbone in Tennessee)
- Farm Bureau chapters — the agriculture angle
- Liberty/sovereignty groups
- Local talk radio hosts who cover these issues
- Church networks (Fritts in TN was a Liberty University graduate; Fesi in LA was a Baptist deacon — faith communities are a natural base)

**SkyLedger's role:**
SkyLedger itself is a tool in this fight. The flight tracker, the evidence database, the facts page — these are resources no other state effort has had. Consider:
- Creating an Arkansas-specific page on SkyLedger showing flights over Arkansas
- Adding a "Bring a Ban to Your State" page with the universal playbook
- Using the evidence submission system to collect Arkansas-specific observations

#### 1.3 Research Arkansas-Specific Angles

Gather ammunition specific to Arkansas:

- [ ] **FOIA the governor's office** for any correspondence about weather modification authority (the 2020 expansion of emergency powers)
- [ ] **FOIA the Arkansas Department of Energy and Environment** for any weather modification permits, applications, or communications
- [ ] **Check USASpending.gov** for any federal weather modification contracts involving Arkansas
- [ ] **Check NOAA's database** for any historical weather modification reports touching Arkansas
- [ ] **Research what's happening in neighboring states** — Texas has active cloud seeding. Missouri, Mississippi, Oklahoma, Louisiana have all had legislative activity. Arkansas is surrounded.

### Phase 2: Draft and Refine (September–December 2026)

**Goal: Have a polished bill and a committed sponsor ready to pre-file.**

#### 2.1 Draft the Bill

Start with Zero Geoengineering's model legislation and adapt for Arkansas:

**Key decisions:**

1. **Scope**: Ban everything (Tennessee model) or carve out agricultural exemptions (Louisiana model)?
   - **Recommendation: Ban everything.** Arkansas has no active cloud seeding, so there's no industry to oppose the bill. The cleaner the bill, the harder it is to water down.

2. **Penalties**: Misdemeanor (TN: $10K/day) or felony (FL: $100K + 5 years)?
   - **Recommendation: Start with the felony-level Florida model.** If there's pushback, you can negotiate down. Starting low leaves no room to negotiate up. Louisiana's two-bill strategy showed that having an aggressive bill fail makes the moderate one look reasonable.

3. **Reporting mechanism**: Include a citizen reporting system (FL/LA model)?
   - **Recommendation: Yes.** This creates ongoing public engagement and feeds data into SkyLedger's evidence system. Model it after Florida's DEP reporting requirement.

4. **Committee pathway**: Agriculture or Public Health?
   - **Recommendation: Agriculture, Forestry & Economic Development.** The farmland protection framing is strongest in Arkansas and this committee will have the most sympathetic members.

5. **Constitutional basis**: Tenth Amendment (TN) or state sovereignty (FL)?
   - **Recommendation: Both.** Include Tenth Amendment language AND frame around protecting Arkansas's sovereignty over its airspace and agricultural resources.

#### 2.2 Build the Pressure Campaign

**September 2026:**
- Stand for Health Freedom action alert goes live for Arkansas
- Begin collecting email addresses through SkyLedger and coalition partners
- Draft template emails/calls for supporters to send to key legislators

**October–November 2026:**
- Host informational events (in-person or online) in key districts — focus on the sponsor's district and committee members' districts
- Get local talk radio coverage
- Begin social media campaign with Arkansas-specific content

**December 2026:**
- Pre-file the bill
- Press release with sponsor quote focused on environmental protection and state sovereignty
- Notify all coalition partners to activate their networks

### Phase 3: Session (January–March 2027)

**Goal: Pass the bill in 60 days.**

#### Week 1-2: Committee Referral
- Bill gets referred to committee
- Activate email campaign: target every member of the committee
- **Volume target: 5,000+ emails minimum** (Tennessee hit 11K, Florida hit 100K)

#### Week 3-4: Committee Hearing
- **Pack the hearing room** with supporters
- Testimony lineup (in this order):
  1. **Sponsor** — introduces the bill, frames it as environmental protection and state sovereignty
  2. **Physician or scientist** — discusses the White House OSTP report, health implications of atmospheric aerosols, the regulatory vacuum
  3. **Farmer/rancher** — "I need to know what's falling on my land and my livestock"
  4. **Citizen** — personal observations, why this matters to everyday Arkansans
- Distribute a one-page fact sheet to every committee member with:
  - The White House OSTP report citation
  - The three states that have already passed laws
  - The fact that Arkansas has zero protections
  - The bill's key provisions

#### Week 5-8: Floor Votes
- Continue email/call pressure on all members, not just committee
- **Prepare for the mockery** — if a Democrat tries a joke amendment (like Tennessee's Sasquatch amendment), let it fail and move on. Don't engage.
- Have the sponsor prepared with clean responses:
  - "If this isn't happening, the bill costs nothing. If it is, we're protected."
  - "Three states have already done this. We're not the first, and we won't be the last."
  - "The federal government's own report says they're researching this. We're saying not in Arkansas."

#### Week 9-10: Governor's Desk
- If it passes both chambers, lobby the governor's office
- Frame for Sanders: "This is consistent with your challenge to EPA overreach. You've already established that the federal government doesn't get to experiment on Arkansas."
- She has no political reason to veto.

### Phase 4: After Passage

- Document the entire process for other states
- Add Arkansas to SkyLedger's legislation tracker
- Build the reporting mechanism (if included in the bill) into SkyLedger
- Connect with the next state in line and share the playbook
- Consider follow-up legislation to strengthen enforcement (but wait at least one session — Tennessee's enforcement follow-up failed when pushed too soon)

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Can't find a willing sponsor | Low (81 R House members, 29 R Senators) | Approach multiple legislators simultaneously; lead with the data, not the conspiracy |
| Bill gets watered down in committee | Medium | Have governor signal support early (FL model); be willing to negotiate on penalties but not scope |
| Industry opposition (cloud seeding) | Very low (no AR programs) | Point to NOAA data showing zero AR activity |
| Media ridicule | High but irrelevant | It helped in TN, FL, and LA. Don't seek mainstream approval. |
| Low constituent engagement | Medium | This is why Stand for Health Freedom's platform matters — it does the heavy lifting |
| Bill dies in committee | Low if pressure is sustained | Committee members facing thousands of constituent emails don't table bills |
