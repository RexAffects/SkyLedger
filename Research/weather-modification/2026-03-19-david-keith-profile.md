## 2. His Role in Geoengineering

### Research Contributions

David Keith has worked on solar geoengineering since 1992, when he and Hadi Dowlatabadi published one of the first assessments of the technology and its policy implications. He is widely considered the most prominent and influential academic advocate for solar geoengineering research in the world.

**Key contributions:**
- Co-authored one of the earliest academic assessments of solar geoengineering (1992)
- Led development of Harvard's Solar Geoengineering Research Program (SGRP), the largest academic program of its kind
- Co-initiated the SCoPEx (Stratospheric Controlled Perturbation Experiment) project
- Published over 200 academic papers with 20,000+ citations
- Authored the book *A Case for Climate Engineering* (MIT Press, 2013)
- Developed an edX energy course reaching 150,000+ students globally
- Built hardware including the first atom interferometer and a Fourier-transform spectrometer used by NASA

**What he proposes:**
- Injecting sulfuric acid (or other aerosols like calcium carbonate) into the upper stratosphere to create a reflective particle layer between Earth and the Sun
- This would partially reflect incoming sunlight, theoretically reducing global temperatures
- He frames this as a "complement" to emissions reductions, not a replacement

**His stated position:**
- **Advocates for research**, not deployment
- Supports an international moratorium on large-scale deployment until the science is thoroughly understood and governance frameworks are in place
- Proposes a "two-threshold" system: deployment moratoria combined with regulated small-scale research
- Argues that ignorance is more dangerous than knowledge -- if society doesn't research now, decisions may be made later "in ignorance and in a rush"
- Has stated he would support a near-term moratorium on deployment

### Overview

| **Sole funder** | Bill Gates, from his personal funds |
| **Total disbursed** | ~$8.5 million (as of last public reporting) to 13 research projects and scientific meetings |

### How It Works

- Gates gives personal gifts to Harvard University
- Keith and Caldeira select which projects receive funding
- Two people control all allocation decisions for the entire fund
- Focus: "early-stage innovative research where relatively small and timely grants can significantly advance understanding"

### Known Grant Recipients

|-----------|------------|--------|---------------|

| Various | Multiple | $240K | Travel and workshops for geoengineering advocates |

### FICER Policy on Field Testing

FICER states it "has not supported and will not support any field tests of methods that introduce new kinds of interference into the climate system" (e.g., solar radiation management, ocean fertilization). It does support field testing of industrial processes for carbon removal.

### Criticism of FICER

- **ETC Group** (geoengineering watchdog) called Gates "the sugar daddy of geoengineering" and criticized the fund's concentration of power: "the same small group working on high-risk technologies that will geoengineer the planet is also trying to engineer the discussion around international rules and regulations"
- Nearly half the fund went to Keith and Caldeira themselves
- Two individuals control all funding decisions with no public governance structure
- The fund straddles a line: Gates personally funds it, but it is formally separate from the Gates Foundation, creating a layer of distance

## 4. Connection to Bill Gates

The Gates-Keith connection is deep and multi-layered:

1. **FICER:** Gates personally funds FICER ($8.5M+), which Keith co-manages and from which he has received $4.6M+

## 5. Harvard SCoPEx Project

### What It Was

The **Stratospheric Controlled Perturbation Experiment (SCoPEx)** was an experiment designed to measure the behavior of aerosols in the stratosphere. Initiated by David Keith and Frank Keutsch at Harvard.

- **Goal:** Release a small amount of calcium carbonate (chalk dust) or sulfate particles from a high-altitude balloon into the stratosphere and measure how they disperse, react, and scatter light
- **Scale:** Extremely small -- would release less than 2 kg of material
- **Purpose:** Generate real-world data to test computer models of solar geoengineering
- **First proposed publicly:** 2017, at a summit in Washington, DC

### Timeline of Events

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2017 | Keith and Keutsch publicly present SCoPEx plans |
| 2017-2020 | Advisory committee established; extensive review process |
| March 2021 | Planned test flight in Kiruna, Sweden (using Swedish Space Corporation balloon) |
| March 2021 | Saami Council issues protest letter calling it a "real moral hazard" |
| April 2021 | Swedish Space Corporation suspends the test flight |
| June 2021 | Saami Council formally petitions Harvard to shut down the project |
| August 2023 | Research team conveys to Advisory Committee that work is suspended |
| Spring 2023 | David Keith departs Harvard for University of Chicago |
| March 2024 | Frank Keutsch announces he is no longer pursuing SCoPEx |
| March 2024 | Project officially abandoned following Advisory Committee recommendation |

### Why It Was Cancelled

Multiple reinforcing factors:

1. **Indigenous opposition:** The Saami Council, representing Indigenous Saami peoples of Scandinavia, objected to aerial experiments above their territory. The Saami have historical trauma from the 1986 Chernobyl disaster, which deposited radiation on their villages and grazing areas
2. **Environmental group opposition:** Multiple organizations argued geoengineering is too dangerous to experiment with
3. **Advisory committee:** Recommended delaying until meaningful public engagement was conducted
4. **Keith's departure:** His move to University of Chicago in 2023 removed one of the two principal investigators
5. **Governance gap:** No framework existed for who gets to authorize stratospheric experiments
6. **Critical media coverage and activist pressure** complicated the project's ability to move forward

## 6. Move from Harvard to University of Chicago

### When

April 2023

### What He Left

- Gordon McKay Professor of Applied Physics (SEAS)
- Professor of Public Policy (Kennedy School)
- Leadership of Harvard's Solar Geoengineering Research Program

### What He Gained

- Professor of Geophysical Sciences
- Founding Faculty Director, Climate Systems Engineering (CSE) Initiative
- Commitment from UChicago to hire up to 10 new faculty members for the initiative
- Partnership with Argonne National Laboratory
- Access to Aurora supercomputer for climate modeling

### Why He Moved

Keith has not publicly stated a single decisive reason. What is publicly known:

- **UChicago offered a much larger institutional commitment** -- a new initiative with up to 10 faculty hires, compared to his existing program at Harvard
- **SCoPEx was stalled** at Harvard, facing opposition and governance challenges
- **UChicago's brand** emphasizes open debate and tackling controversial questions -- a potential better fit for politically contentious research
- Keith's own statement: "My only confident assertion about climate engineering is that open collaborative research today is vital to inform decisions our children must make tomorrow"
- Harvard's Solar Geoengineering Research Program continues under the Salata Institute without Keith

**Note:** The Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists characterized the initiative as potentially "a brave research program or a potentially dangerous foray into solar geoengineering."

## 8. Conflicts of Interest

### Keith's Self-Disclosed Conflicts

Keith maintains a public conflict of interest statement on his website (davidkeith.earth/conflict-of-interest-statement/). Key disclosures:

- **Carbon Engineering involvement ended** November 2023 with sale to Occidental
- **Stopped academic DAC research** after founding Carbon Engineering, to avoid conflicts between his company and his academic work
- **Does not patent solar geoengineering technologies** -- a deliberate choice to avoid financial incentives around deployment
- **Harvard SGRP policy:** Rejected donations from fossil fuel-associated donors
- **Consulting:** Minimal -- one Shell project in early 2000s; occasional speaking fees
- **Charitable giving:** Donates to environmental organizations (Sea Shepherd, The Wilderness Society, NRDC, EDF)

### The Tension Critics Identify

Despite Keith's disclosures, critics point to structural issues:

1. **$72 million from Carbon Engineering sale** -- even if he donates most of it, he personally profited from a technology (DAC) closely related to his geoengineering advocacy
2. **Carbon Engineering was funded by fossil fuel companies** (Chevron, Occidental, BHP) and an oil sands billionaire (N. Murray Edwards) -- entities with strong interest in technologies that could allow continued fossil fuel use
3. **FICER self-funding** -- Keith and Caldeira directed ~$4.6M of Gates' money to their own research, out of $8.5M total
4. **Small network controls the field** -- ETC Group and others note that a small group of interconnected researchers (Keith, Caldeira, and their associates) simultaneously conduct the research, manage the funding, advise the governance bodies, and shape the policy debate
5. **Moral hazard argument** -- Clive Hamilton and others argue that regardless of Keith's intent, his research creates commercial interests that will eventually lobby for deployment
6. **Sold to Big Oil** -- Carbon Engineering was acquired by Occidental Petroleum, one of the largest oil producers. Keith said he was uncomfortable with this but it happened anyway

### Keith's Counter-Arguments

- His geoengineering advocacy actually *hurts* his company's interests: if solar geoengineering weakens climate policy, it threatens demand for Carbon Engineering's DAC technology
- He deliberately does not patent solar geoengineering tech
- He stopped academic DAC research to maintain a firewall
- He rejects fossil fuel money for his solar geoengineering research (but Carbon Engineering accepted it for DAC)

## 9. Published Positions

### Summary of Keith's Stated Views

- **Solar geoengineering should be researched** -- aggressively and openly
- **Deployment should be under a moratorium** until governance frameworks exist
- **It is NOT a replacement for emissions reductions** -- it is a "complement"
- **Ignorance is more dangerous than knowledge** -- not researching means future decisions will be made in crisis
- **He opposes any commercial work on solar geoengineering** -- wants it kept in the public/academic domain
- **He supports direct air capture as a commercial technology** -- and is proud to have commercialized it

### Key Publication

*A Case for Climate Engineering* (MIT Press / Boston Review Books, 2013) -- his book-length argument for why solar geoengineering research should proceed.

## 10. Controversy and Criticism

### From Scientists

- **Raymond Pierrehumbert** (Oxford atmospheric physicist): Called solar geoengineering "a grave threat to human civilization" and said even *researching* it is "actively dangerous"
- **60 scientists and scholars** (January 2022): Signed an open letter demanding an international non-use agreement on solar geoengineering, including a ban on outdoor experiments and prohibition on national funding
- **400+ researchers** (early 2025): Signed an open letter calling for a pause on all atmospheric intervention experiments until governance frameworks are established
- **Clive Hamilton** (ethicist/author): Argued that once research creates commercial interests, those interests will inevitably lobby for deployment regardless of researchers' intentions

### From Environmental Groups

- **David Suzuki** (Canadian environmentalist): Called geoengineering "arrogant and simplistic"
- **Al Gore**: Called solar geoengineering "insane, utterly mad and delusional in the extreme"
- **ETC Group** (geoengineering watchdog, Montreal): Criticized the concentration of power, noting "the same small group working on high-risk technologies that will geoengineer the planet is also trying to engineer the discussion around international rules and regulations"
- **Saami Council**: Opposed SCoPEx as contrary to their respect for nature and a "real moral hazard"

### From Critics of the Funding Model

- Diana Bronson (ETC Group): Highlighted that a small group of advocates controls both the research and the governance conversations
- Clive Hamilton: Pointed out that N. Murray Edwards (oil sands billionaire) invested in Keith's startup, creating a direct financial link between fossil fuel interests and geoengineering research

## Summary Assessment

David Keith is the single most influential academic advocate for solar geoengineering research globally. He occupies a unique position at the intersection of research, policy advisory, commercial enterprise, and funding management in this field.

**Key facts for transparency purposes:**

1. He made approximately $72 million from a company (Carbon Engineering) funded by fossil fuel companies and an oil sands billionaire, then acquired by Occidental Petroleum
2. He co-manages a fund (FICER) where he and one colleague control all allocation decisions for Bill Gates' personal geoengineering money, and directed roughly half to their own work
3. He simultaneously conducts research, manages funding, advises governance bodies, and shapes policy in the same field
4. He advocates for research but not deployment -- a position that keeps the field growing while deferring the politically hardest questions
5. He has been more transparent about conflicts than most -- maintaining public disclosures, not patenting solar geoengineering tech, and rejecting fossil fuel money for his solar geoengineering work (while accepting it for his carbon capture company)
6. The field he works in has attracted serious scientific opposition (400+ researchers calling for a pause) and criticism from prominent environmentalists including Al Gore and David Suzuki
