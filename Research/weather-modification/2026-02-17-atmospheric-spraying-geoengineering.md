# Research: Atmospheric Spraying — What's Real, What's Not, and What to Do About It

## The Question

Are they spraying stuff in our skies? Is it harmful? And what can a concerned citizen do about it — peacefully, legally, and effectively?

## Honest Assessment: Separating Signal from Noise

### What IS Real and Confirmed

1. **Cloud seeding is happening right now.** At least 9 US states actively seed clouds: California, Colorado, Idaho, Utah, Wyoming, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas, and North Dakota. Utah runs the largest remotely-operated generator network in the country. These programs use **silver iodide (AgI)**, potassium iodide, dry ice, and liquid propane.

2. **The military did it in war.** Operation Popeye (1967-1972) was a classified cloud-seeding campaign over Vietnam — 2,600+ sorties dropping silver and lead iodide on the Ho Chi Minh Trail. Authorized by Kissinger/CIA. The Secretary of Defense **lied to Congress** about it. Only exposed because a journalist broke the story.

3. **Geoengineering research is real.** Harvard's SCoPEx experiment proposed injecting aerosols into the stratosphere to block sunlight. It was cancelled in 2024 without ever testing. But the research continues. A new ARIA-funded project plans to send mineral dust into the stratosphere via weather balloons.

4. **There is almost no oversight.** NOAA tracks weather modification activities but has **no regulatory authority**. The Weather Modification Reporting Act of 1972 requires reporting, but that's it — no approval process, no environmental impact review required. State regulation is a patchwork.

5. **Silver iodide concerns are legitimate but understudied.** At current levels it appears safe, BUT: it decreases photosynthetic activity in phytoplankton, reduces soil bacteria viability, and freshwater organisms are sensitive to it. The GAO's own 2025 report admits: *"It is not known whether more widespread use of silver iodide would have an effect on public health or the environment."* They literally don't know.

6. **Massive legislative pushback is already happening.** Tennessee became the first state to ban geoengineering in 2024 (signed by Gov. Bill Lee). Florida followed in 2025. As of 2025, **lawmakers in 30 states** have proposed geoengineering/cloud seeding bans. 22 states introduced bills in 2025 alone.

### What's NOT Well-Supported

1. **Covert large-scale spraying from commercial aircraft** — No credible evidence supports this. The logistics of secretly modifying thousands of commercial planes, involving tens of thousands of mechanics and pilots who all stay silent, doesn't hold up.

2. **Grid patterns as proof of spraying** — Grid patterns in the sky are explained by the National Airspace System's flight lanes. Aircraft flying different routes at different altitudes create crosshatch patterns. Whether contrails persist or vanish depends entirely on humidity at altitude. On high-humidity days, contrails spread into haze. On dry days, they disappear in seconds. Same planes, different atmospheric conditions.

3. **Aluminum/barium/strontium being systematically deployed from planes** — Soil and water testing that shows these metals doesn't distinguish between natural geological sources, industrial pollution, and alleged aerial spraying. These metals are naturally present in soil and vary hugely by region.

### The Honest Middle Ground

Rex — here's where I land as your thinking partner: **There are REAL programs modifying our atmosphere with chemicals, and they operate with shockingly little oversight or public consent.** That's not conspiracy — that's documented fact. The government literally lied about doing it during Vietnam. Cloud seeding is happening today with chemicals whose long-term effects "are not known" per the GAO.

But the specific claim that commercial jets are secretly spraying heavy metals in grid patterns — the evidence doesn't support that. What you're likely seeing on those hazy days are persistent contrails spreading in humid upper-atmosphere conditions.

**The good news:** You don't NEED the commercial-jets theory to be true for your concern to be valid. The confirmed programs alone — cloud seeding with understudied chemicals, geoengineering research moving toward deployment, zero meaningful public consent — are MORE than enough to fight against. And the fight is already gaining real momentum.

## Key Insights

- The real battle isn't about contrails — it's about **consent**. Nobody asked the public if they wanted silver iodide seeded into their water supply's source clouds.
- The precedent is terrible. The government literally ran a secret weather warfare program and lied to Congress about it. Of course people don't trust them.
- The legislative movement is massive and growing fast — 30 states. This is not fringe anymore.
- Your instinct is right: **staying close to nature, not assuming we know better than the systems that sustain us** — that's the core principle worth fighting for.

## Peaceful, Legal, Creative Ways to Take Action

### Direct Political Action (Highest Impact)

1. **Support your state's geoengineering/cloud seeding ban bill.** Check if your state has one pending (22 states introduced bills in 2025). If not, contact your state representative and reference Tennessee's SB 2691 and Florida's SB 56 as models.

2. **Attend or submit written testimony to your state legislature.** State hearings on these bills accept public comment. Your personal testimony about wanting clean skies and public consent matters.

3. **Support the NOAA petition for updated reporting rules.** The University of Houston Law Center petitioned NOAA to require environmental impact reporting for weather modification. Public comments strengthen this.

4. **Contact your city/county council.** Local resolutions opposing geoengineering create political pressure from the bottom up. Several cities have passed these.

### Community Building (Multiplier Effect)

5. **Start or join a local clean-skies group.** Focus on the CONFIRMED programs — cloud seeding, geoengineering proposals. This keeps it credible and hard to dismiss.

6. **Organize sky-watching and documentation days.** Photograph and document what you see. Pair it with flight tracking data (FlightRadar24 is free) to show what aircraft are in the area. This builds an evidence base.

7. **Host screenings + discussions.** But curate carefully — lead with the confirmed facts (cloud seeding, Operation Popeye, GAO report) rather than unsubstantiated claims. Credibility is your weapon.

8. **Connect with organic farmers.** They have the most to lose from atmospheric contamination and the most credibility when speaking about it. Build coalitions.

### Legal and Institutional Pressure

9. **File FOIA requests.** Request weather modification activity reports from NOAA (they're required to collect them). Request your state's cloud seeding permits and reports. Make the information public.

10. **Support environmental law organizations** working on atmospheric protection — specifically ones focused on the precautionary principle (don't do it until you PROVE it's safe, not the other way around).

11. **Push for water and soil testing transparency.** Advocate for your local water district to test for silver iodide and publish results. Public data builds public awareness.

12. **Document the lack of consent.** The strongest legal and moral argument is simple: **nobody asked us.** Frame everything around informed consent and the precautionary principle.

### Personal Protection

13. **Water filtration.** A good multi-stage filter (reverse osmosis + activated carbon) removes heavy metals and silver iodide from drinking water. Worth doing regardless.

14. **Grow your own food where possible.** You're already doing mushrooms — expand that. Raised beds with known-source soil. Community gardens.

15. **Support local organic agriculture.** Vote with your dollars for farming that works WITH nature.

### Information and Awareness

16. **Share the GAO report, not documentaries.** When you talk to people, lead with the government's OWN admission that they don't know the long-term effects. That's harder to dismiss than any documentary.

17. **Use the Tennessee/Florida precedent.** "30 states are trying to ban this" is a powerful normalizing statement. It takes this out of "conspiracy" territory and into "legitimate political movement."

18. **Write about it.** Blog, social media, letters to the editor. Focus on consent, precautionary principle, and confirmed programs.

## The Core Principle (for Philosophy)

*We don't have the right to modify the systems that sustain all life without the informed consent of the people who depend on those systems. The atmosphere belongs to everyone. The precautionary principle — prove it's safe BEFORE you do it — should govern any atmospheric modification program.*
