# Weather Modification International (WMI) — Company Profile

**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Type:** Research — Public Sources Only

---

## 1. Company Overview

**Full Name:** Weather Modification, Inc. / Weather Modification International (WMI)
**Headquarters:** 3802 20th Street North, Fargo, ND 58102
**Founded:** 1961 (by Wilbur Brewer, a North Dakota farmer interested in hail suppression)
**Current Ownership:** Patrick H. Sweeney and James P. ("Jim") Sweeney
**Website:** weathermodification.com

WMI is the world's largest private aerial cloud-seeding company. It provides cloud seeding for rain/snow enhancement, hail suppression, fog clearing, atmospheric research, weather radar systems, and aircraft modification for special missions.

---

## 2. Corporate Structure — The Sweeney Family of Companies

The Sweeneys own and operate an integrated ecosystem of companies:

| Company | Role | Location |
|---------|------|----------|
| **Weather Modification LLC** | Cloud seeding operations, atmospheric research | Fargo, ND |
| **Fargo Jet Center (FJC)** | Fixed-base operator, aircraft maintenance, avionics, paint, charter | Fargo, ND (KFAR) |
| **Premier Jet Center (PJC)** | FBO subsidiary | Minneapolis, MN (KFCM) |
| **Ice Crystal Engineering (ICE)** | Manufactures cloud seeding pyrotechnic flares | Kindred, ND |
| **Exclusive Aircraft Sales (EAS)** | Aircraft brokerage and sales | Minneapolis, MN |
| **SPEC Incorporated** | Airborne cloud physics instrumentation (acquired June 2025) | Boulder, CO |

This vertical integration means the Sweeneys control the entire cloud seeding value chain: they build the flares (ICE), modify the aircraft (FJC), fly the missions (WMI), sell/lease the planes (EAS), and now manufacture the atmospheric measurement instruments (SPEC).

## 6. What They Spray

### Primary Agent: Silver Iodide (AgI)
- The core cloud seeding agent used by WMI and the industry broadly
- Molecular structure similar to ice, which helps water crystallize around it
- Delivered via pyrotechnic flares: ejectable (ICE-EJ) and burn-in-place (ICE-EB) variants
- Silver iodide is often burned with acetone as a carrier

### Other Agents Used in Cloud Seeding
- **Calcium chloride (CaCl2)** — hygroscopic agent for warm cloud seeding; ICE manufactures calcium chloride burn-in-place flares
- **Potassium iodide (KI)** — alternative glaciogenic agent
- **Sodium chloride (NaCl)** — hygroscopic seeding agent (table salt)
- **Dry ice (solid CO2)** — historical agent, less common now
- **Propane (C3H8)** — used in some ground-based generators

### Safety Debate
- **Industry position:** Silver iodide exists naturally in the environment at low concentrations. Over 100 Sierra Nevada lakes/rivers studied since the 1980s found no detectable silver above natural background levels.
- **Concerns:** Silver iodide IS regulated under the Clean Water Act as a hazardous substance. Some studies show bioaccumulation potential, particularly for aquatic life. Silver concentrations have exceeded health standards in areas with repeated exposure. The GAO notes it is "not known whether more widespread use of silver iodide would have an effect on public health or the environment."

### Key International Projects
- **Saudi Arabia:** Partnership since 2004 under scientific oversight of NCAR. Saudi Arabia's Regional Cloud Seeding Program launched 2022 ($256 million first year). WMI provided aircraft, training, and technology transfer. Five Beechcraft King Air turboprops ordered for the Saudi program.
- **India (Maharashtra):** $4.5 million cloud seeding program in 2015 — the largest aerial cloud seeding campaign ever attempted in India at the time. WMI trained Indian pilots, meteorologists, and Doppler radar technicians. This project later became the subject of litigation (see Lawsuits section).
- **South Africa (Western Cape):** WMI involvement in feasibility studies around 2018-2019 for cloud seeding over Theewaterskloof Dam, with simulations suggesting 40-50% precipitation increase potential.

## 10. Lawsuits

### Weather Modification LLC v. Brackin (2020)
**Case:** 3:20-cv-00073, U.S. District Court for the District of North Dakota
**Filed:** May 4, 2020
**Judge:** Chief Judge Peter D. Welte

**Background:** Neil Brackin served as president of WMI and board member of Fargo Jet Center. He departed and founded SAHC (Specialty Aviation Holdings Corporation), a competing weather modification company.

**WMI's Claims:**
- Brackin engaged in "secretive, self-dealing, and self-interested" conduct
- Specific allegations centered on the cloud seeding project in Maharashtra, India
- Claims of fraud and deceit; sought exemplary damages

**Brackin's Counterclaims:**
- Defamation — alleged WMI engaged in a campaign to destroy his reputation in the industry
- Sought exemplary damages on defamation claim

**Status:** Preliminary injunction granted June 12, 2020. Case appears to have continued through at least 2021 based on available records.

**No other major lawsuits found** against WMI in public searches. No lawsuits from affected communities or environmental groups regarding cloud seeding effects were identified.

- **Victim:** Hans Ahlness, 55, VP of Operations for Weather Modification Inc.

- **Background:** Ahlness had been with WMI since 1977 (34 years), held a commercial pilot license, was a flight instructor, and received the Weather Modification Association's Distinguished Service Award in 2003.

## 12. Regulatory Relationship with NOAA

### Legal Framework
- **Weather Modification Reporting Act of 1972:** Requires all persons conducting weather modification in the U.S. to report to the Secretary of Commerce at least 10 days prior to and after activities.
- **15 CFR Part 908:** Federal regulation governing recordkeeping and reporting.
- **Penalty for non-reporting:** Up to $10,000 in fines.

### NOAA's Role and Limitations
- NOAA is required to TRACK weather modification activities but has NO AUTHORITY to regulate them.
- NOAA does not fund or participate in cloud seeding projects.
- Reports are submitted via email to weather.modification@noaa.gov.

### GAO Findings (2026)
A GAO report (GAO-26-108013) found significant oversight deficiencies:
- Over half of all reports filed with NOAA likely contain errors
- Missing required information (e.g., maps)
- NOAA has no written agency guidance for reviewing reports or maintaining its database
- Weather modification operators may report inconsistent information or fail to report entirely

This means WMI and other operators essentially self-regulate with minimal federal oversight.

## 14. Lobbying and Political Activity

### Direct Company Activity
- No registered federal lobbyists found directly associated with Weather Modification Inc. in available records.
- Patrick Sweeney recorded $12,150 in political contributions in 2014.

### Industry Association Lobbying
- WMI, ICE, and the North Dakota Atmospheric Resource Board are all members of the **Weather Modification Association (WMA)**, a professional organization promoting "beneficial weather modification."
- The **North Dakota Weather Modification Association (NDWMA)** receives annual funding from county boards and lobbies the state legislature for more weather modification funding. This creates a taxpayer-funded lobbying loop: public money goes to NDWMA, which lobbies for more public spending on weather modification, which goes to WMI.

### Legislative Context
- In 2025, North Dakota SB 2106 (introduced by Sen. Todd Beard) would have banned weather modification and made it a misdemeanor. It was defeated 18-29 in the Senate.
- At the federal level, Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene's proposed "Clear Skies Act" would criminalize cloud seeding entirely.
- The weather modification industry is beginning to engage federal lobbyists as legislative threats grow. (Rainmaker Technology hired Crossroads Strategies for federal lobbying.)

## 15. Community Opposition

### North Dakota Pushback
- **Williams and Mountrail Counties** voted to withdraw from the cloud seeding program in November 2024.
- As of 2025, only McKenzie, Bowman, and parts of Slope County still participate.
- Farmer Doug Stangeland (Williams County): "We are tired of government controlling our weather."
- Farmer Roger Neshem (former county weather modification authority member): Claimed local concerns went unaddressed, including flights outside permitted areas.
- Organizers argue silver iodide is dangerous and claim seeding is not helping farmers.

### Broader Opposition
- A coalition of 200 environmental groups has protested weather modification experiments, arguing the technology does not address root causes of climate change.
- More than 70% of U.S. adults worry that cloud seeding will be deployed before scientists fully understand environmental consequences (Pew Research).
- Transnational concerns: China's massive cloud seeding program raised diplomatic tensions with India, Thailand, and Cambodia (Wilson Center analysis).

## 18. Key Takeaways

1. **Monopoly-like position:** WMI is the world's largest private cloud seeding company and the only one that does "full turnkey" — making the flares, modifying the planes, running the radar, and flying the missions. This vertical integration through the Sweeney family of companies gives them extraordinary market dominance.

2. **Minimal oversight:** NOAA tracks but cannot regulate. GAO found over half of reports likely contain errors. No federal agency actually regulates the safety or environmental impact of cloud seeding operations.

3. **Self-dealing structure in North Dakota:** The Sweeneys own all three entities that receive the state's cloud seeding contracts — WMI (operations), Fargo Jet Center (aircraft), and ICE (chemicals). This creates no competitive pressure on pricing.

4. **Growing opposition:** Counties are withdrawing, state legislators are proposing bans, and federal legislation to criminalize cloud seeding has been introduced. The industry is responding by hiring federal lobbyists.

5. **Environmental questions remain open:** The GAO states flatly that it is "not known whether more widespread use of silver iodide would have an effect on public health or the environment." Meanwhile, the scale of operations continues to expand globally.

6. **The India/Brackin litigation** reveals internal corporate conflict and allegations of fraud in connection with international contracts, suggesting the business practices of WMI leadership have been challenged even from within.
