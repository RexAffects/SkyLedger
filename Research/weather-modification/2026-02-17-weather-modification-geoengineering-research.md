# Weather Modification, Cloud Seeding & Geoengineering Research

**Date:** 2026-02-17
**Purpose:** Factual research separating confirmed programs, proposed programs, and unsubstantiated claims.

---

## 1. Cloud Seeding Programs in the United States

### CONFIRMED: Active Programs

Cloud seeding is real, decades-old, and actively practiced. According to the GAO (January 2025 report), **nine U.S. states** currently operate active cloud seeding programs:

- **California** - Primarily winter snowpack enhancement
- **Colorado** - Winter snowpack, Colorado River Basin cooperation
- **Idaho** - Winter snowpack
- **Utah** - Largest remotely-operated generator network in the US; exploring drone-based seeding
- **Wyoming** - Winter snowpack
- **Nevada** - Precipitation enhancement
- **New Mexico** - Precipitation enhancement
- **Texas** - Rainfall enhancement (warm cloud seeding)
- **North Dakota** - Hail suppression and rain enhancement

Some sources extend this list to 11 states, adding **Kansas** and **Oklahoma**.

Activity is concentrated in western states that rely on snowpack for water supply. The Colorado River Basin states finalized a cost-sharing agreement in 2018 that extends through fall 2026.

### Who Authorizes Them

- State-level agencies (e.g., Utah Division of Water Resources, Texas Weather Modification Program)
- Water districts and utilities fund many programs
- Some programs are county-level or managed by water conservancy districts
- Federal funding has historically come through the Bureau of Reclamation

### Chemicals Used

**Primary agents:**
- **Silver iodide (AgI)** - The most common. Efficient ice nucleating properties; crystal structure mimics ice.
- **Potassium iodide (KI)** - Similar to silver iodide, considered less toxic; used in environmentally sensitive areas.
- **Dry ice (solid CO2)** - Used in aircraft-based seeding; rapidly cools cloud moisture to form ice crystals.
- **Liquid propane (C3H8)** - Expands into ice nuclei in supercooled clouds; used for targeted, short-duration work.

**Additional agents:**
- Sulfur dioxide (SO2)
- Bismuth tri-iodide (BiI3)
- Calcium chloride (CaCl2) - Hygroscopic; promotes droplet coalescence in warm clouds
- Sodium chloride (table salt) - Hygroscopic seeding agent

### Oversight (See Section 7 for Full Legal Framework)

NOAA is **required to track** weather modification activities but has **no authority to regulate** them. Federal reporting is required but the GAO found it may not include all necessary information.

## 2. Geoengineering / Stratospheric Aerosol Injection (SAI) Research

### STATUS: Research-stage. No deployment has occurred. No real-world SAI tests have been conducted.

### Harvard's SCoPEx Program - CANCELLED

- **What it was:** The Stratospheric Controlled Perturbation Experiment (SCoPEx), led by Professors David Keith and Frank Keutsch. Designed to release small amounts of aerosol (calcium carbonate) from a high-altitude balloon to measure stratospheric behavior.
- **What happened:** The project faced years of delays. A planned 2021 test flight in Kiruna, Sweden was halted after backlash from the Saami Council (indigenous group). The experiment became a proxy for the broader debate about whether geoengineering research should proceed.
- **Current status:** Professor Keutsch announced in **March 2024** that he is no longer pursuing the experiment. SCoPEx is **officially cancelled**. The balloon platform may be repurposed for unrelated stratospheric research.
- **Key quote:** "The experiment became this proxy for a kind of debate about whether solar geoengineering research should move forward." - David Keith

### Harvard's Ongoing Research (Post-SCoPEx)

Harvard's Salata Institute continues **theoretical and modeling** research on geoengineering:
- Ansar Lemon's team modeled visual impacts of SAI (sky whitening, sunset changes) for sulfate, calcium carbonate, and diamond aerosols
- Research on low-altitude (13 km), high-latitude (60 N/S) injection strategies
- Research on who could technically deploy SAI (feasibility with existing aircraft)

### New Natural Materials Project (2025+)

- Led by Hugh Hunt in collaboration with Harvard and Imperial College London
- Funded by the **Advanced Research + Invention Agency (ARIA)**
- Will send small amounts of **natural mineral dust** into the stratosphere via weather balloons
- Launches planned from US and/or UK sites
- This is **atmospheric science research**, not a deployment program

### Funding Sources for SAI Research

- Harvard's Solar Geoengineering Research Program (now under Salata Institute)
- Previously funded by Bill Gates, the Hewlett Foundation, and others
- ARIA (UK government research agency) for the natural materials project
- Various university grants and government research funding

### IMPORTANT DISTINCTION

No SAI deployment has ever occurred. All work remains at the research, modeling, and (cancelled) small-scale experiment stage. The gap between "researching SAI" and "deploying SAI" is enormous -- comparable to the gap between studying nuclear physics and building a reactor.

## 3. Weather Modification History - Confirmed Programs

### Operation Popeye (1967-1972) - CONFIRMED, DECLASSIFIED

- **What:** Military cloud-seeding program during the Vietnam War
- **Objective:** Extend the monsoon season over the Ho Chi Minh Trail to soften roads, cause landslides, and disrupt North Vietnamese supply lines
- **How:** Silver iodide and lead iodide dispersed from C-130 Hercules and F-4C Phantom aircraft based at Udon Thani Royal Thai Air Force Base, Thailand
- **Scale:** Over 2,600 cloud seeding sorties from 1967-1972, primarily over Laos
- **Authorization:** Allegedly sponsored by Secretary of State Henry Kissinger and the CIA. Secretary of Defense Melvin Laird denied to Congress that the program existed.
- **Slogan:** "Make Mud, Not War" (54th Weather Reconnaissance Squadron)
- **Exposure:** Journalist Jack Anderson revealed its existence in 1971, triggering public outcry and Senate hearings
- **Consequence:** Directly led to the ENMOD Treaty (see Section 7)
- **Goal:** Extend rainfall by 30-45 days per monsoon season

### Project Stormfury (1962-1983) - CONFIRMED, DECLASSIFIED

- **What:** Joint U.S. Department of Commerce / U.S. Navy program to weaken hurricanes
- **How:** Silver iodide seeding of hurricane eyewalls to disrupt internal structure
- **Results:** The hypothesis was ultimately proven incorrect. Most hurricanes don't contain enough supercooled water for seeding to be effective. Unseeded hurricanes undergo the same structural changes expected from seeded ones.
- **Last experimental flight:** 1971 (lack of candidate storms)
- **Officially cancelled:** 1983

### Project Cumulus (1949-1952) - CONFIRMED, BRITISH

- UK government cloud seeding experiments for military advantage
- Abandoned after the 1952 Lynmouth flood (34 deaths), though no evidence directly linked the project to the disaster

### Other Confirmed U.S. Weather Modification History

- **1946:** Vincent Schaefer at General Electric performs first cloud seeding experiment (dry ice)
- **1947:** Bernard Vonnegut (brother of Kurt) discovers silver iodide as a cloud seeding agent
- **1950s-present:** Civilian cloud seeding programs expand across western states
- **Bureau of Reclamation:** Funded Project Skywater and other research programs through the 1970s-80s

## 4. Contrails vs. Persistent Contrails - The Science

### What Contrails Are

Contrails (condensation trails) are line-shaped clouds produced by aircraft engine exhaust at cruising altitudes. They are composed primarily of **water in the form of ice crystals**. The combination of water vapor in engine exhaust and low ambient temperatures causes their formation.

### Three Types

1. **Short-lived contrails:** Form and evaporate in seconds to minutes. The surrounding atmosphere has **low humidity**.
2. **Persistent non-spreading contrails:** Last minutes to hours but remain narrow. Atmosphere has **moderate humidity**.
3. **Persistent spreading contrails:** Last hours to over a day, spread laterally, and can become indistinguishable from natural cirrus clouds. The atmosphere is **supersaturated** (humidity above 100% relative to ice).

### Why Grid Patterns Form

The grid/crosshatch patterns have a straightforward explanation:

- **Flight lanes:** The U.S. National Airspace System uses a gridlike network of airways (jet routes, Victor airways). Jets fly north-south, east-west, and at various angles.
- **Crossing altitudes:** Planes at different altitudes on crossing routes produce intersecting trails.
- **Wind spreading:** High-altitude winds (often 90+ mph) blow trails laterally, causing them to spread and create broader patterns.
- **Persistence conditions:** When the atmosphere at altitude is supersaturated, ALL contrails in that region persist and spread. You see many trails because many flights pass through the same atmospheric conditions.

### Why Some Days Have Many and Others Have None

This is purely a function of **atmospheric humidity at altitude**. On dry days, contrails vanish in seconds. On humid, supersaturated days, every flight leaves a persistent, spreading trail. Weather conditions at ground level tell you nothing about conditions at 30,000-40,000 feet.

### Climate Impact of Contrails (Real Concern)

Persistent contrails and contrail-induced cirrus clouds have a **measurable warming effect**. This is an active area of legitimate climate science research. Google and American Airlines have run pilot programs to reroute flights to avoid creating persistent contrails.

The documentary presents claims but does not distinguish between:
- Confirmed cloud seeding programs (real)

## 6. Health and Environmental Concerns - Silver Iodide

### What the Science Says

**Current levels appear safe, but long-term widespread use is understudied.**

### Toxicity

- Silver iodide has an NFPA 704 health hazard rating of **2** (out of 4), meaning it can cause temporary incapacitation or possible residual injury with intense or chronic exposure
- The EPA has determined silver is **not classifiable as a human carcinogen**
- Concentrations from current cloud seeding operations remain below human health, aquatic life, and terrestrial life standards

### Environmental Impact Studies

- **Freshwater organisms** are more sensitive than soil organisms to AgI
- Exposure at reference monitoring levels (0.43 uM) caused **significant decrease in photosynthetic activity** in phytoplankton and moderate decrease in soil bacteria viability
- AgI from repeated cloud seeding **may moderately affect biota** in both terrestrial and aquatic ecosystems if seeding is repeatedly applied in a specific area and large amounts accumulate

### The Key Gap (from GAO 2025 Report)

**"It is not known whether more widespread use of silver iodide would have an effect on public health or the environment."**

This is the most important finding: current levels appear safe, but we don't have adequate research on what happens if cloud seeding scales up significantly.

### Other Environmental Concerns

- Precipitation redistribution: seeding in one area may reduce rainfall downwind (unproven but theoretically plausible)
- Difficulty measuring actual effectiveness vs. natural variation
- Potential liability questions if seeding causes flooding

## 7. Legal Framework - Weather Modification Regulation

### Federal Law

**Weather Modification Reporting Act of 1972 (Public Law 92-205)**
- Requires anyone conducting weather modification to **report** to NOAA at least 10 days prior
- NOAA must maintain public records of all reported activities
- **Critical limitation:** NOAA can track but **cannot regulate** weather modification

**15 CFR Part 908** - Federal regulations implementing the reporting requirements

### International Law

**ENMOD Convention (1977/1978)**
- Full name: Convention on the Prohibition of Military or Any Other Hostile Use of Environmental Modification Techniques
- Bans weather warfare -- military use of environmental modification with "widespread, long-lasting or severe effects"
- Approved by UN General Assembly December 10, 1976 (96-8 vote, 30 abstentions)
- U.S. Senate ratified it 98-0 on November 28, 1979
- Entered into force for the U.S. on January 17, 1980
- **Does NOT ban** civilian weather modification (cloud seeding for water supply, etc.)

### State-Level Regulation

States have a patchwork of laws. Some require permits, some require reporting, some have minimal oversight:
- **Utah:** Detailed permitting through Division of Water Resources
- **Texas:** Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation oversees weather modification permits
- **Colorado, Wyoming:** State engineer or water board oversight
- Many states have **no specific regulations** for weather modification

### Public Oversight Mechanisms

- NOAA maintains a **public database** of weather modification project reports (viewable online)
- Records must be made publicly available "to the fullest practicable extent"
- Anyone can submit a written request to NOAA to inspect records
- GAO audits (e.g., the January 2025 report) provide independent assessment

### 2024 Petition to Update Regulations

In March 2024, the University of Houston Law Center's Environment, Energy & Natural Resources Center petitioned NOAA to **amend reporting regulations** to:
- Account for solar radiation modification experiments
- Include environmental impact reporting
- NOAA has requested public comment on this petition

## 8. Recent Legislative Efforts

### Growing State-Level Movement

As of 2025, **lawmakers in 30 U.S. states** have proposed bills to ban or restrict geoengineering and/or cloud seeding. In 2025 alone, **22 states** introduced bills.

### Laws Enacted

| State | Year | Details |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Tennessee** | 2024 | First state to ban geoengineering (signed by Gov. Bill Lee) |
| **Florida** | 2025 | Senate Bill 56, "landmark geoengineering ban" prohibiting weather modification including cloud seeding and solar radiation modification. Gov. DeSantis announced intent to sign May 6, 2025 |

### Bills Introduced (Not Yet Enacted as of Research Date)

States with proposed legislation include: Illinois, Kentucky, Minnesota, New Hampshire, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Dakota, Montana, and many others.

**New Hampshire HB1700** specifically targeted stratospheric aerosol injection, cloud seeding, and other "polluting atmospheric" activities with penalties and cross-border notice provisions.

## 9. Tennessee's Anti-Geoengineering Bill - Deep Dive

### The Original Law (2024)

- **Bill:** HB 2063 / SB 2691
- **Passed:** April 2024 (House and Senate)
- **Signed:** By Republican Governor Bill Lee
- **First in the nation** to enact such a ban

### What It Prohibits

The ban prohibits the **"intentional injection, release, or dispersion, by any means, of chemicals, chemical compounds, substances, or apparatus within the borders of the state into the atmosphere with the express purpose of affecting temperature, weather, or intensity of the sunlight."**

This is broad -- it covers:
- Cloud seeding
- Stratospheric aerosol injection
- Any intentional atmospheric modification for weather/climate purposes

### 2025 Follow-Up: HB 1112

Tennessee strengthened the ban with HB 1112, adding **new penalties** to reinforce the prohibition on weather modification and geoengineering.

### Context and Controversy

- Committee hearings produced a "confusing mix of truth, innuendo and fiction"
- Many supporters conflated geoengineering with chemtrails conspiracy theories
- Scientists noted there is no evidence of large-scale covert geoengineering programs currently operating
- The law effectively bans activities (like large-scale SAI deployment) that don't currently exist
- It also bans activities (like cloud seeding) that ARE real but that Tennessee wasn't actively conducting

## Summary: What's Real, What's Proposed, What's Not Supported

### CONFIRMED REAL

- Cloud seeding programs in 9-11 U.S. states (decades of operation)
- Operation Popeye (military weather modification, Vietnam War, declassified)
- Project Stormfury (hurricane modification research, declassified)
- Silver iodide, dry ice, potassium iodide used as seeding agents
- Persistent contrails caused by atmospheric supersaturation (well-understood physics)
- NOAA reporting requirements for weather modification (but no regulatory authority)
- ENMOD Treaty banning military weather warfare (U.S. ratified 98-0)
- GAO finding that cloud seeding effectiveness is 0-20% additional precipitation and often statistically indistinguishable from zero
- Growing state legislative movement to ban geoengineering (30 states, 2 enacted)

### PROPOSED / RESEARCH STAGE

- Stratospheric Aerosol Injection (SAI) - extensively modeled, never deployed
- Harvard SCoPEx experiment - cancelled before any test occurred
- Natural mineral dust stratospheric experiments (ARIA-funded, upcoming)
- Updated NOAA reporting to cover geoengineering experiments
- Expanded environmental impact studies on silver iodide

### NOT WELL-SUPPORTED BY EVIDENCE

- Claims of covert, large-scale chemical spraying programs ("chemtrails")
- Claims that contrail grid patterns indicate anything other than normal air traffic + atmospheric conditions
- Claims that aluminum, barium, or strontium are being systematically sprayed from commercial aircraft
- Claims that weather modification programs are responsible for specific weather events (drought, flooding) beyond their documented operational areas
