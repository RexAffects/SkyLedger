# Geoengineering & Weather Modification: Legal Landscape

**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Purpose:** Current lawsuits, legislation, organizations, and ways to support legal efforts against geoengineering and weather modification.

---

## 1. Active Lawsuits & Legal Actions

### The GeoFight Coalition — Federal Litigation in Preparation

The most significant coordinated legal effort is **The GeoFight** ([thegeofight.com](https://thegeofight.com/)), a coalition led by three attorneys:

- **Nicole Pearson** — Constitutional attorney, founder of FACTS LAW TRUTH JUSTICE (human and civil rights firm, 15+ years litigation experience)
- **Blake Horwitz** — Chief partner of The Blake Horwitz Law Firm, Chicago, IL (decades of litigation experience)
- **Tom Renz** — Ohio-based attorney known for constitutional challenges

**Their approach:**
- Collecting court-admissible soil, water, and air samples tested by independent labs to prove contamination
- Assembling expert witnesses: doctors, atmospheric scientists, meteorologists
- Seeking whistleblowers: pilots (military, commercial, private), USAF personnel, employees of NASA, NOAA, NWS, DOE
- Building toward federal lawsuit(s) to set nationwide precedent

**Status:** Pre-filing stage. They are fundraising and building the evidentiary foundation. No federal case has been filed yet as of this research date.

### ICAN (Informed Consent Action Network) — FOIA Actions

ICAN has filed FOIA requests to uncover geoengineering research funding. Key findings so far:

- Obtained documents showing the **National Science Foundation (NSF) awarded ~$400,000 in 2021** to Cornell and Indiana University scientists for stratospheric aerosol injection (SAI) research — described as the "holy grail" of geoengineering
- The funded scientists claimed their climate model simulations would solve "choosing where and when to inject aerosols"
- ICAN's legal team is pursuing documents the NSF is withholding related to a workshop with "policy experts to discuss results and future directions"
- ICAN is expanding beyond its vaccine focus to address government-sponsored geoengineering

**Source:** [ICAN Press Release](https://icandecide.org/press-release/ican-documents-show-us-government-funded-holy-grail-of-geoengineering-research/)

### Geoengineering Watch / Dane Wigington

Dane Wigington and Geoengineering Watch have been involved in legal efforts including:

- A lawsuit filed to expose the EPA for allegedly awarding $190 million in taxpayer grants to "independent" scientists to get desired air quality test results (referenced throughout 2025-2026 on their site)
- Wigington testified as an expert witness for the **Wyoming geoengineering ban bill** (October 2025)
- **Past case:** Wigington v. MacMartin — a defamation suit Wigington brought against a climate scientist. The motion to dismiss and anti-SLAPP motion were granted (case did not succeed)

**Source:** [Geoengineering Watch Legal Action](https://geoengineeringwatch.org/category/legal-action/)

### No Major Successful Court Rulings Yet

As of March 2026, there have been **no landmark court rulings** specifically on geoengineering or weather modification legality. The legal battle is primarily being fought through **legislation** rather than litigation. The evidentiary challenges (proving causation, proving specific programs exist) have made court cases difficult.

## 2. International Legal Framework

### Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD) — De Facto Moratorium

- **2008:** CBD adopted a moratorium on ocean fertilization
- **2010:** Expanded to a de facto moratorium on ALL geoengineering (with exceptions for small-scale scientific research)
- **2016:** Countries reaffirmed the moratorium
- **2024 (COP16):** Moratorium reaffirmed again due to concerns over proliferation of outdoor experiments, especially marine

**Key limitation:** The CBD moratorium is politically binding but not legally enforceable. It has "intent but not capacity" to enforce.

### UN Environmental Modification Treaty (ENMOD)

Many geoengineering techniques have latent military applications. Deployment could violate ENMOD, which prohibits the hostile use of environmental modification.

### Failed UN Resolution Attempts

- At the **UN Environment Assembly (UNEA) in Nairobi**, the African Group of countries pushed to ban solar radiation management, while Switzerland pushed for an expert research panel
- Countries could not reach consensus — status quo (moratorium) continues
- The **U.S. has blocked UN resolutions** on geoengineering regulation

**Source:** [Scientific American — U.S. Blocks U.N. Resolution on Geoengineering](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/u-s-blocks-u-n-resolution-on-geoengineering/)

### Harvard SCoPEx Cancellation

Harvard's Stratospheric Controlled Perturbation Experiment (SCoPEx) was **postponed indefinitely** in April 2021 after the Swedish Space Corporation pulled out under pressure from the Saami Council (indigenous people). This is the most prominent example of public/indigenous opposition blocking a research program.

## 3. State Legislation — Laws Enacted

### Tennessee (FIRST STATE) — Signed April 11, 2024
- **Bill:** SB 2691 / HB 2063
- **What it does:** Prohibits intentional injection, release, or dispersion of chemicals into the atmosphere to affect temperature, weather, or sunlight intensity
- **Penalties:** Class C misdemeanor, fines up to $10,000/day per violation
- **Effective:** July 1, 2024
- **Vote:** Senate 25-6, House 70-22
- **Source:** [Tennessee Legislature](https://wapp.capitol.tn.gov/apps/BillInfo/default.aspx?BillNumber=SB2691&GA=113)

### Florida — Signed May 2025
- **Bill:** Senate Bill 56
- **What it does:** Prohibits weather modification including cloud seeding and solar radiation modification. Creates a **public reporting system** — DEP must establish email/online form for citizens to report suspected activities. Publicly owned airports must report monthly (starting Oct 1, 2025) any aircraft equipped for geoengineering
- **Penalties:** **Felony** — up to 5 years in prison and fines up to $100,000
- **Vote:** House 82-28
- **Effective:** July 1, 2025
- **Significance:** First state to **criminalize** geoengineering as a felony
- **Source:** [Governor DeSantis Press Release](https://www.flgov.com/eog/news/press/2025/governor-ron-desantis-celebrates-action-protect-floridians-chemical-and)

### Montana — Passed March 6, 2025
- **Bill:** Senate Bill 473
- **What it does:** Bans stratospheric aerosol injection and solar radiation management
- **Exception:** Permits cloud seeding for water resource management
- **Source:** [State-Level Geoengineering Bans](https://statecapitallobbyist.com/environment/state-level-geoengineering-bans-florida-montana-and-beyond/)

### Louisiana — Signed into law ~June 2025
- **Bill:** SB 46 / HB 608
- **Sponsors:** Sen. "Big Mike" Fesi (R), Rep. Kim Coates (R)
- **What it does:** Bans intentional release of chemicals into the atmosphere to alter weather, temperature, climate, or block sunlight
- **Penalties:** $200,000 fine per violation
- **Vote:** House 58-33
- **Notable:** Louisiana DEQ has collected **over 400 citizen reports** since the law went into effect
- **Source:** [Louisiana Legislature coverage](https://lailluminator.com/2025/05/19/weather-modification-bills/)

## 4. State Legislation — Bills Introduced (Not Yet Enacted)

As of early 2026, **lawmakers in approximately 30 U.S. states** have proposed bills to ban or restrict geoengineering. Key pending bills:

| State | Bill | Key Provisions | Penalties |
|-------|------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Pennsylvania** | SB 508 (March 2025) | "Clean Air Preservation Act" — bans cloud seeding, solar radiation modification | Felony, fines $500,000+, 2 years imprisonment |
| **North Carolina** | HB 362 (March 2025) | Prohibits atmospheric modification including SAI and cloud seeding | Environmental Management Commission develops enforcement |
| **New Jersey** | SB 4161 (Feb 2025) | Bans release of hazardous chemicals for geoengineering | $10K first offense, $25K second, $50K subsequent |
| **Rhode Island** | HB 5217 (Jan 2025) | Bans geoengineering and weather modification including SAI and cloud seeding | TBD |
| **Iowa** | Senate Study Bill 3010 (Jan 2026) | Bans cloud seeding, weather engineering, polluting atmospheric activity | Class D felony, up to 5 years, $1,025-$10,245 fines per day |
| **Wyoming** | Draft bill (Oct 2025) | Committee advanced ban on unauthorized atmospheric geoengineering + resolution to Congress. Failed to advance 10-year cloud seeding moratorium (tie vote) | TBD |

## 5. Federal Legislation

### Clear Skies Act — H.R. 4403 (119th Congress, 2025-2026)
- **Sponsor:** Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene (R-GA)
- **Introduced:** July 2025
- **What it does:**
  - Prohibits weather modification through geoengineering, cloud seeding, and solar radiation management
  - Repeals any federal statute authorizing weather modification
  - Nullifies any federal regulation or executive order authorizing weather modification
  - Creates a **public reporting system** — Americans can report suspicious activity, EPA required to investigate
- **Penalties:** Felony — up to $100,000 fines and/or 5 years in prison per violation. Additional civil penalties of $10,000 per violation from EPA and FAA
- **Status:** Introduced, referred to committee
- **Source:** [Congress.gov — H.R. 4403](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/4403/text)

### Congressional Hearing — September 2025
- **Title:** "Playing God with the Weather — a Disastrous Forecast"
- **Committee:** House Subcommittee on Delivering on Government Efficiency (DOGE subcommittee)
- **Chair:** Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene
- **Duration:** Nearly 2 hours of testimony
- **Focus:** Federal government's role in weather control and geoengineering, ethical and legal issues, transparency of taxpayer spending
- **Source:** [House Oversight Committee](https://oversight.house.gov/release/hearing-wrap-up-subcommittee-demands-transparency-of-government-weather-and-climate-engineering/)

### Weather Act Reauthorization Act of 2025 — H.R. 3816
- Modernizes weather forecasting and research
- Bipartisan legislation that passed committee unanimously
- **Source:** [Congress.gov — H.R. 3816](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/3816)

### GAO Report — Federal Oversight Gaps
The Government Accountability Office found that the federal government:
- Does NOT have sufficient oversight over weather modification activities
- Is "not fully meeting its responsibilities to maintain and share weather modification reports"
- **Source:** [GAO Report](https://www.gao.gov/assets/gao-25-107328.pdf)

## 7. How Citizens Can Help Beyond Donating

1. **Report suspected activity** — In states with laws (FL, LA, TN), use official reporting channels. Florida has a dedicated DEP email and online form.
2. **Contact state legislators** — With 30 states introducing bills, your state may have pending legislation. Testify at hearings, submit written comments.
3. **Contact federal representatives** — Support H.R. 4403 (Clear Skies Act) or similar federal bills.
4. **Whistleblower outreach** — If you know pilots, military personnel, or government agency employees with knowledge, connect them with The GeoFight legal team.
5. **Independent testing** — Some organizations accept independently collected soil, water, and air samples for testing.
6. **Share information** — Awareness is growing rapidly; the jump from 1 state law (2024) to 4+ state laws and 30 states with bills (2025-2026) shows momentum.

---

## 8. Key Takeaways

1. **The legal fight is primarily legislative, not litigation.** Courts have been difficult terrain (evidentiary challenges, proving causation). The strategy has shifted to passing state bans and building toward federal legislation.

2. **Momentum is real and accelerating.** Tennessee was alone in 2024. By early 2026, 4 states have enacted laws and ~30 have introduced bills. A federal bill exists and a congressional hearing has been held.

3. **The GeoFight coalition represents the most serious litigation effort** but is still in preparation. No major federal lawsuit has been filed yet.

4. **International governance is weak.** The CBD moratorium is politically binding but unenforceable. The U.S. has actively blocked stronger UN action.

5. **Florida's law is the strongest** — felony charges, mandatory airport reporting, public complaint system. Louisiana's $200,000 fine per violation is the steepest financial penalty.

6. **The GAO has confirmed** that federal oversight of weather modification is inadequate, which strengthens the case for legislation and legal action.
