# Who Is Involved: Weather Modification, Cloud Seeding & Geoengineering Directory

**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Purpose:** Comprehensive directory of named companies, individuals, government agencies, academic institutions, funders, and organizations involved in weather modification and geoengineering programs. All information is from publicly documented, verifiable sources.

---

## 1. U.S. Government Agencies

### Federal Agencies

**NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration)**
- Required by the Weather Modification Act of 1976 to track weather modification activities
- Does NOT fund, participate in, or oversee cloud seeding operations
- Has NO regulatory authority over weather modification
- Maintains a database of weather modification activity reports (often incomplete -- GAO found over half of filed reports contain errors)
- NOAA's Earth's Radiation Budget (ERB) Program supports research relevant to solar geoengineering
- Contact for reports: weather.modification@noaa.gov

**Bureau of Reclamation (Dept. of Interior)**
- Historically funded cloud seeding through "Project Skywater" (1960s-1980s)
- In 2023, granted $2.4 million to the Southern Nevada Water Authority for Upper Colorado River Basin cloud seeding
- Grant funded upgrading manual generators to remote-operated ones and aerial seeding
- Had largely backed off funding in recent years but returned citing new research supporting effectiveness

**DOE (Department of Energy)**
- Participates in interagency coordination on solar geoengineering research
- Co-hosted a 2022 workshop with NOAA on Marine Cloud Brightening research gaps
- Part of the OSTP-led interagency working group on solar radiation modification

**EPA (Environmental Protection Agency)**
- As of 2025, conducting internal review of authorities that could be used to regulate geoengineering
- Sent a formal request to Make Sunsets (April 15, 2025) regarding their SO2 balloon releases
- Has authority under Marine Protection, Research and Sanctuaries Act (MPRSA) for ocean-based activities
- Determining whether new congressional authority is needed or another agency should lead
- Maintains geoengineering information pages: epa.gov/geoengineering

**OSTP (White House Office of Science and Technology Policy)**
- Released a congressionally mandated report on Solar Radiation Modification (June 30, 2023)
- Directed by Congress (Consolidated Appropriations Act 2022) to develop a five-year federal research plan
- Working with NOAA, NASA, NSF, and DOE to form an interagency working group on SRM governance

**DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency)**
- Co-funded HAARP (High-frequency Active Auroral Research Program) beginning in 1990
- Participated in weather modification coordination during the 1970s
- Runs ICE (Ice Control for cold Environments) program
- Runs ACTM (AI-assisted Climate Tipping-point Modeling) program
- SilverLining's research director Alex Wong previously worked at DARPA

**DOD (Department of Defense)**
- Historical: Operation Popeye (1967-1972) -- classified cloud seeding to extend monsoons in Vietnam
- Published "Weather as a Force Multiplier: Owning the Weather in 2025" (1996 Air Force paper)
- No publicly documented current operational weather modification programs
- Subject to the UN ENMOD Convention (1978) prohibiting hostile use of environmental modification

**NASA**
- Partners with UAE on cloud seeding research
- Partners with National Centre for Atmospheric Research (NCAR)
- Part of the OSTP interagency working group on solar geoengineering

**NSF (National Science Foundation)**
- Part of the OSTP interagency coordination on geoengineering research
- Funds atmospheric science research at universities

**GAO (Government Accountability Office)**
- Published GAO-25-107328: "Cloud Seeding Technology: Assessing Effectiveness and Other Challenges" (January 2025)
- Published GAO-26-108013: "Weather Modification: NOAA Should Strengthen Oversight to Ensure Reliable Information"
- Found that NOAA lacks written guidance for reviewing reports or maintaining its database

### State Agencies and Officials

**Texas**
- Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR) -- administers licensing and permits under the Texas Weather Modification Act
- Multiple active cloud seeding programs across the state
- South Texas Weather Modification Association (STWMA), based in Pleasanton -- operates programs targeting ~6 million acres
- Edwards Aquifer Authority (San Antonio) -- has been a consistent cloud seeding sponsor for 24+ years
- Panhandle Groundwater Conservation District -- runs precipitation enhancement programs

**Idaho**
- Idaho Water Resource Board (IWRB) -- facilitates and provides funding support
- Idaho Department of Water Resources -- administers the Cloud Seeding Program
- Collaborative program with Idaho Power Company (see Private Companies)

**Utah**
- Utah Division of Water Resources -- authority under the Cloud Seeding Act of 1973
- Multi-decade programs with newer drone and ground generator methods
- Spends ~$1.5 million annually on cloud seeding

**Wyoming**
- Wyoming Water Development Office -- administers the Wyoming Weather Modification Program
- Operational cloud seeding began in 2014 over the Wind River Mountain Range
- 10-year viability study conducted 2004-2014

**Colorado**
- Colorado Department of Natural Resources -- customer of Rainmaker
- Spends ~$1.5 million annually on cloud seeding

**Nevada**
- Desert Research Institute (DRI) -- runs cloud seeding since the 1960s
- Nevada Legislature provides DRI $600,000/year since 2023
- Southern Nevada Water Authority -- sent $800,000 to Utah's program; accepted $2.4M federal grant

**North Dakota**
- North Dakota Cloud Modification Project (NDCMP) -- hail damage reduction and rain enhancement
- Operated in western ND, including Bowman and part of Slope counties

**California**
- Multiple cloud seeding programs, primarily winter snowpack enhancement

**New Mexico**
- Recently began approving permits for warm weather seeding

### States Banning Geoengineering

- **Tennessee** -- First state to adopt a ban (April 2024), signed by Gov. Bill Lee
- **Florida** -- Passed ban in June 2025 on acts affecting temperature, weather, or sunlight intensity
- **Montana** -- Adopted restrictions (March 2025)
- **22 states** introduced bills in 2025; **30 states total** have proposed SRM bans
- Federal "Clear Skies Act" introduced to criminalize weather modification nationally

### Geoengineering / Solar Radiation Management Companies

**Make Sunsets**
- **Founded:** October 2022
- **Co-founders:** Luke Iseman (CEO, former Y Combinator director of hardware) and Andrew Song
- **Location:** Based in U.S.; initial operations conducted in Mexico (halted by Mexican government)
- **Technology:** Releases balloons filled with sulfur dioxide (SO2) into the stratosphere
- **Business model:** Sells "cooling credits" at $10/credit
- **Funding:** $1.2+ million from Boost VC, Pioneer Fund, and Draper Associates (Tim Draper)
- **Initial seed:** $750,000 from Boost VC and Pioneer Fund
- **SO2 released:** ~0.1 tons as of May 2025
- **EPA action:** Received formal EPA request (April 15, 2025) regarding activities

**Stardust Solutions**
- **Founded:** Early 2023
- **Incorporation:** U.S.-incorporated, Israeli-based
- **CEO/Co-founder:** Yanai Yedvab (former deputy chief scientist, Israel Atomic Energy Commission)
- **Co-founder:** Amyad Spector (nuclear physicist, formerly Israeli government)
- **Lead Scientist:** Eli Waxman (astrophysicist, Weizmann Institute of Science)
- **Funding:** $60 million in venture capital raised
- **Goal:** Build solar geoengineering system by 2030
- **Plans:** "Outdoor contained experiments" releasing reflective particles from modified aircraft at ~11 miles altitude
- The most heavily funded private geoengineering startup as of 2025

## 9. Key Patterns and Observations

1. **The funding network is small and interconnected.** A core group of ~7 philanthropic organizations and ~11 billionaires/billionaire-founded philanthropies fund the majority of solar geoengineering research. Many have ties to technology and financial capital sectors (Microsoft, Hewlett-Packard, Google, Skype, hedge funds).

2. **Cloud seeding is operational and routine; geoengineering is experimental.** Cloud seeding has been practiced for 75+ years across 50+ countries. Stratospheric aerosol injection and marine cloud brightening remain in the research/early-testing phase.

3. **Governance is fragmented.** No single federal agency has clear regulatory authority over geoengineering. The EPA is actively trying to determine if it has authority. States are filling the gap with bans (30 states have proposed bills).

4. **The private sector is accelerating.** Stardust ($60M), Rainmaker ($25M), and Make Sunsets are commercializing what was previously academic. Stardust plans outdoor tests in 2025.

5. **NOAA's reporting system is broken.** Over half of filed reports contain errors. No written agency guidance exists for reviewing reports. The GAO has called for reform.

6. **Military history is documented.** The U.S. military has a proven track record of weaponizing weather (Operation Popeye), leading to the 1978 ENMOD Convention ban on hostile use.

7. **Opposition is growing.** 30 states have proposed geoengineering bans. Indigenous groups and environmental organizations actively resist. SCoPEx was cancelled partly due to indigenous opposition in Sweden.
