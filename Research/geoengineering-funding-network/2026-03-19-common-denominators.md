# Common Denominators — The Geoengineering Funding and Influence Network

**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Topic:** Connecting the dots between funders, researchers, lobbyists, and for-profit ventures

---

## The Core Pattern

A small group of billionaires and hedge funds have built an interconnected system where they simultaneously:

1. **Fund the nonprofit research** that produces favorable models
2. **Invest in for-profit startups** positioned to deploy the technology
3. **Lobby Congress and governments** for public funding of deployment
4. **Advise on governance** of the field they're invested in

If governments eventually deploy solar geoengineering at scale ($5-10 billion/year), the early investors and patent holders capture enormous value. The entire pipeline — from research to policy to deployment — is controlled by overlapping actors.

## The Science Gap

- The research underlying geoengineering is **almost entirely modeling-based**
- **SCoPEx** (Harvard's stratospheric experiment, the one real-world test) was **cancelled in March 2024** after years of delays and opposition
- No one has demonstrated controlled, measured, verified aerosol cooling at any scale
- The models say it could work. The models are funded by people who need it to work. There is no independent experimental verification.

---

## The Governance Vacuum

- In the US, companies need only **file a one-page form with the Commerce Department 10 days before releasing aerosols**
- No environmental review required
- No community notification required
- No independent scientific review required
- SilverLining tested marine cloud brightening without telling the city of Alameda
- Make Sunsets launched in Mexico without telling Mexico
- The governance framework essentially does not exist

## What This Means

This is not a conspiracy theory — it's a structural analysis. Every individual fact is publicly documented. The pattern is:

1. **Extreme concentration:** ~11 billionaires and a handful of hedge funds control most of the funding
2. **Systematic conflicts of interest:** The same people research, fund, patent, invest in, lobby for, and advise on governance of geoengineering
3. **No independent verification:** The science is modeling-based, funded by interested parties, with no experimental confirmation
4. **No governance:** There is effectively no regulatory framework preventing unilateral deployment
5. **Massive potential profit:** If deployment happens at scale ($5-10B/year of government spending), early movers capture enormous value

The question is not whether these individuals believe in what they're doing — most of them probably genuinely believe geoengineering could help. The question is whether a system where the same people occupy every role — funder, researcher, patent holder, investor, lobbyist, and governance advisor — can produce trustworthy outcomes on a decision that affects everyone on the planet.
