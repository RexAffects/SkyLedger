## 6. Revolving Door: Government <-> Geoengineering

### CONFIRMED Connections (not necessarily "revolving door" in the traditional sense)

| Person | Government Role | Geoengineering Role |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Ken Caldeira** | Edward Teller Fellow at Lawrence Livermore National Lab (DOE weapons lab), 1991-2005 | FICER co-manager, Breakthrough Energy Senior Scientist, Stanford courtesy professor |
| **Kelly Wanser** | Consultant to US Dept. of State on climate technology; Lawrence Livermore Lab | SilverLining Executive Director; helped advance National Academy of Sciences study; introduced Congressional legislation |
| **David Keith** | Adviser to government bodies in US & Canada | Led Harvard SGRP; now leads UChicago CSEi; co-managed FICER |

### Government Programs Shaped by Private Advocacy

- SilverLining "helped advance" the National Academy of Sciences study on SRM and "introduced funding and legislative proposals in the U.S. Congress"
- In 2022, Congress directed OSTP to develop a 5-year SRM research plan -- this followed advocacy by SilverLining and allied organizations
- NOAA received $4M for SRM research starting FY2020
- The White House OSTP released a congressionally mandated SRM report in June 2023

### Governance Gap (CONFIRMED)

- A GAO report found the federal government "does not have sufficient oversight over weather modification activities"
- Companies only need to fill out a **one-page form** with the Commerce Department **10 days** before releasing aerosols into the atmosphere (1970s law)
- Rainmaker (Thiel-backed) is actively lobbying against state "Clear Skies Act" legislation that would criminalize atmospheric chemical releases for weather modification

**Assessment:** The traditional "revolving door" pattern is less pronounced than in defense or pharma. Instead, the pattern is more accurately described as **philanthropic capture of government research agendas** -- private foundations fund the research, shape the expert community, then that community advises Congress and agencies.

## 11. Financial Incentives: How Geoengineering Becomes Profitable

### Carbon/Cooling Credits Market

- **Make Sunsets** sells "cooling credits" -- claiming 1g of SO2 released offsets the warming of 1 ton of CO2 for one year
- A US patent exists (US20120117003A1) for a "Geoengineering Method of Business Using Carbon Counterbalance Credits" -- manufacturing light-scattering nanoparticles, deploying them, receiving credits, and selling credits for income
- The number of companies selling carbon credits based on geoengineering is growing

### Government Contracts

- **Stardust Solutions** business model: nations will pay it "more than a billion dollars a year" to launch aircraft for stratospheric aerosol injection
- **Rainmaker** has contracts with Utah and Colorado Departments of Natural Resources, Santa Barbara Public Works
- **Weather Modification International** has contracts across 35+ countries, partnerships with US Department of Commerce, Naval Surface Warfare Center

### Venture Capital Profit Model

- If governments choose to deploy SAI, they become **dependent on the companies** with proprietary technology
- Stardust Solutions is developing a **proprietary, patented aerosol particle** -- creating vendor lock-in
- SAI annual costs estimated at $5-10 billion/year -- massive recurring revenue for whichever companies control the technology
- Lowercarbon Capital frames this as "buying more time" -- one of three investment pillars alongside emissions reduction and carbon removal

### The Profit Loop (CONFIRMED pattern)

1. Billionaires fund nonprofit research at elite universities
2. Research produces models showing SRM "could work"
3. Same billionaires' VC firms invest in for-profit startups
4. Nonprofits (SilverLining) lobby Congress for public funding and favorable regulation
5. Startups develop proprietary technology and sell credits/contracts
6. If governments adopt SRM, the funders' investments become enormously valuable

**Key example:** Chris Sacca donates to Harvard SGRP (nonprofit research), funds SilverLining (advocacy), AND leads the $60M investment in Stardust Solutions (for-profit deployment). He occupies every seat at the table.

## 12. Is the Science Peer-Reviewed and Replicated?

### What Exists

- **Computer models** are the primary tool for studying SRM. Most research is modeling-based.
- David Keith and colleagues have published peer-reviewed papers since 1992
- Harvard's SCoPEx was designed to collect empirical data to "improve the fidelity of computer model simulations" -- it was cancelled in March 2024 before gathering any atmospheric data
- The Simons Foundation's $50M program focuses on "modeling and lab studies"

### What Does NOT Exist

- **No large-scale empirical testing** of stratospheric aerosol injection has been conducted
- **No replication** of real-world deployment effects
- Current models "may make the simulations look too good" (acknowledged by researchers)
- Models presuppose governance mechanisms that don't exist
- No empirical data on long-term effects of sustained aerosol injection

### Critical Assessments

- "It is irresponsible to talk about deploying stratospheric aerosol injection without fundamental research about its impacts"
- SRM "may pose significant environmental and societal risks, including stratospheric warming, ozone depletion and changes in rainfall, thereby affecting water resources and agriculture"
- The National Academies report "minimizes governance concerns by recommending swift U.S. action"
- "Not enough is known about SRM systems and their potential impacts to allow informed decisions by policymakers" (Simons Foundation's own language)
- Greta Thunberg, the Saami Council, and the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences board all criticized SCoPEx

### Assessment

**The science justifying deployment is almost entirely modeling-based.** The one major experiment designed to bridge the gap between models and real-world data (SCoPEx) was cancelled. Yet for-profit startups are already selling credits and planning deployments. The science has NOT kept pace with the commercial activity.

## Summary: Who Benefits If Geoengineering Gets Normalized?

### Tier 1: Direct Financial Beneficiaries (CONFIRMED)
- **Stardust Solutions** investors (Lowercarbon Capital, Exor/Agnelli family) -- $60M invested, billion-dollar government contract aspiration
- **Rainmaker** investors (Lowercarbon, Starship, Long Journey) -- $50M+ raised, government water contracts
- **Make Sunsets** investors (Boost VC, Pioneer Fund, Draper Associates) -- cooling credit sales
- **Weather Modification International** (Sweeney family) -- 60+ years of government contracts, 35+ countries
- **Patent holders** of SAI and carbon credit methods

### Tier 2: Structural Beneficiaries
- **Hedge fund managers** whose wealth funds the research that could create a new multi-billion-dollar annual market
- **Universities** that receive grants and build departments around SRM (Harvard, UChicago, UW, Cornell)
- **Think tanks and NGOs** (SilverLining, Breakthrough Institute, EDF) whose funding and relevance depend on SRM remaining a live issue
- **Carbon credit market operators** who benefit from a new category of tradeable credits

### Tier 3: Systemic Beneficiaries
- **Fossil fuel industry** -- SRM is explicitly framed as "buying time," reducing pressure for rapid decarbonization
- **Tech/finance capital** -- maintains the economic status quo while appearing to address climate change
- **"Green capitalism" ideology** -- geoengineering validates the premise that technological innovation + markets can solve climate change without systemic change
