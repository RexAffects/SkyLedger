# Florida's Path to a Geoengineering Felony Ban — The Playbook

**What they did, how they did it, and what other states can learn.**

---

## The Timeline

| Date | What Happened |
|---|---|
| November 2024 | Sen. Ileana Garcia (R-Miami) files SB 56 |
| February 2025 | First committee stop — Appropriations Committee on Agriculture/Environment approves **10-2** |
| March 24, 2025 | Senate Rules Committee advances |
| April 2, 2025 | DeSantis posts video on X endorsing SB 56 |
| April 3, 2025 | **Full Senate passes** |
| April 2025 | **Full House passes 82-28** |
| June 20, 2025 | **DeSantis signs into law** |
| July 1, 2025 | **Law takes effect** |

**Filing to law: ~7 months. Active legislative push: ~5 months.**

## The Angle That Worked

Garcia did NOT frame this as conspiracy. She framed it as a **regulatory gap**:

> "There is a lot of unauthorized activity that is currently not regulated both at a federal and a state level, and this is where we wanted to start."

> "The purpose of this bill is to separate fact from fiction."

Senate President Ben Albritton backed this framing:

> "I have heard the conspiracy theories out there, but the fact is we should not be shutting down legitimate concerns. Healthy skepticism is important."

> "There's a lot we don't know in this field of science and people are rightfully concerned."

**This is the key insight: Don't argue about whether it's happening. Argue that there's no oversight, no regulation, and no transparency. Let the lack of answers make the case.**

DeSantis sealed it with a freedom framing:

> "The Free State of Florida means freedom from governments or private actors unilaterally applying chemicals or geoengineering to people or public spaces."

RFK Jr. (HHS Secretary) also endorsed it publicly:

> "Banning geoengineering is a movement every MAHA needs to support."

## What the Law Actually Does

1. **Prohibits** the injection, release, or dispersion of chemicals or substances into the atmosphere for the purpose of affecting temperature, weather, or sunlight intensity
2. **Third-degree felony** — up to **5 years imprisonment** and **$100,000 fine**
3. **Airport reporting requirement** — publicly owned airports must submit monthly reports on any aircraft equipped to disperse substances with climate-altering capabilities. Failure = loss of state funding.
4. **Citizen complaint system** — Department of Environmental Protection accepts citizen reports of suspected geoengineering via email. DEP investigates and can refer violations to Dept. of Health or Division of Emergency Management.
5. **Aircraft operators/controllers** — up to $5,000 fine and 5 years imprisonment

## What Generated Public Support

### The Testimony That Moved Legislators

- **Marla Maples** (Trump's second wife) testified about Alzheimer's connections to geoengineering
- Citizens testified about "unusual trails and streaks" they routinely see
- Multiple people referenced **"The Dimming"** documentary (Dane Wigington / Geoengineering Watch — 25M+ views)
- Testimony about aluminum, barium, and silver iodide in the atmosphere

### The Opposition

- **Augustus Doricko, CEO of Rainmaker** — spoke against SB 56 in committee, arguing it would "deprive farmers in arid regions from getting access to water from cloud seeding"
- **Rep. Anna Eskamani (D)** — concerned the bill was "feeding into conspiracy theories" and urged legislating "on science"
- Despite opposition, the bill passed **82-28 in the House** and **28-9 in the Senate** — strong bipartisan margins

## What Other States Can Learn

### 1. Frame it as a regulatory gap, not a conspiracy
Garcia's genius was positioning the bill as "let's regulate what's unregulated" rather than "they're spraying us." This gave moderate legislators cover to vote yes.

### 2. Governor support is a force multiplier
DeSantis publicly endorsing on social media before the vote created momentum. If you can get the governor on board early, the path clears.

### 3. Citizen testimony creates urgency
The volume of public testimony from concerned citizens showed legislators this wasn't fringe — their constituents cared. Sky Ledger's citizen reports could serve this same function.

### 4. Reference other states
Garcia cited 32 other states with similar legislation. Tennessee being first made Florida's vote easier. Each state that passes makes the next one easier.

### 5. The "Dimming" documentary is a gateway
Multiple testifiers referenced it. It's the most accessible introduction to the topic for people who haven't heard of it. 25M+ views.

### 6. Include an enforcement mechanism
Florida didn't just ban it — they created airport reporting requirements and a citizen complaint system. This gives the law teeth.

### 7. Make it bipartisan
82-28 in the House. 28-9 in the Senate. Both Republicans and Democrats voted yes. Garcia (R) sponsored. This is not a partisan issue.

### 8. Expect industry opposition
Rainmaker's CEO personally testified against it. The weather modification industry will show up. Be prepared with data.

---

## What Arkansas Can Take From This

1. **Find an Ileana Garcia** — one state senator or representative willing to file it
2. **Use the regulatory gap framing** — "There is unauthorized activity not regulated at the federal or state level"
3. **Reference Florida, Tennessee, Montana** — they already did it, we're just catching up
4. **Bring citizen evidence** — Sky Ledger reports from Arkansas constituents
5. **Push for felony-level penalties** — Florida set the standard
6. **Include airport reporting and a citizen complaint system** — gives the law teeth
7. **Get the governor's attention early** — public endorsement accelerates everything
